What it does

See it. Fix it. Prove it.

The work of running a club breaks down into three motions. Most systems do one. ClubThread is the first designed to do all three, in that order.

i.
See it

Cross-domain visibility that no single-vendor dashboard can give you. A member's declining F&B spend, skipped events, and unresolved complaint appear as one signal, not three disconnected tabs.

ii.
Fix it

Intervention routed to the right staff member at the right moment, with the context they need to act. The Membership Director sees the at-risk flag before the resignation letter arrives.

iii.
Prove it

Every recommendation, every intervention, every outcome is measured and fed back. Board-ready reports write themselves from a log that documents what the club did and why.

For the technical evaluator

Built on durable sessions, not another database.

ClubThread is built on Anthropic's Managed Agents — an agent platform where every member and every staff role gets a durable session that persists for the life of the relationship.

The architecture is Layer 3: above your Jonas, ForeTees, Toast, or Clubessential deployment, never a replacement for any of them. Vendor integrations are read-only MCP servers with credentials held in isolated per-club vaults. No Swoop service ever sees a vendor token.

Every signal that matters is an event on a session log. Every decision has a reasoning chain. Every real-world action requires explicit staff confirmation. The system coordinates. Humans act.

Questions GMs ask

The four questions, answered honestly.

Will this require replacing Jonas or ForeTees?

No. ClubThread sits above your existing stack and never writes to any source system. Your Jonas deployment, your ForeTees tee sheet, your Toast POS all stay exactly where they are. ClubThread reads from them to build context and routes actions back to your staff, who complete bookings and updates in the systems they already use. Most pilots are live in two weeks with zero migration.

How much control do I have over what ClubThread does on its own?

Complete control. ClubThread never books, charges, staffs, sends, or modifies state in a source system without explicit staff confirmation. Every suggested action queues for review before anything happens in the real world. You choose what auto-executes — and you can start with nothing on auto and unlock categories one at a time as the track record builds. Many of our pilot GMs keep most actions manual for the first quarter. That's how it should work.

What happens when a staff member leaves?

Their session, and the institutional memory inside it, transfers cleanly to the incoming role. The new Membership Director arrives on day one with eighteen months of context — every decision, every intervention, every member relationship that matters — available to read. Turnover stops being a moment where the club resets.

How does this help me prove ROI to my board?

Every recommendation surfaced, every intervention approved, every outcome measured — all of it writes to an append-only event log. The Board Report Compiler assembles a monthly packet from that log: "On May 14 we caught an understaffing risk and added one server. Measured impact: $2,400 in covers saved, zero complaints filed." Dollar impact traced to action, action traced to signal. The story of what the club did and why is traceable, not reconstructed.
