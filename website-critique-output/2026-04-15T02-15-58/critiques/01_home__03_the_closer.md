## The Conversion Audit

Because this is a single, long-form landing page, I am auditing its core functional sections based on their job in the funnel.

**1. The Hero Section (The Hook)**
*   **The Job:** Answer "What is this, who is it for, and why should I care right now?" (Fogg's Motivation).
*   **Is it doing it?** Yes. "Your tee sheet, POS, and CRM each see part of the picture. Swoop assembles it into one 6 AM brief." is brilliant. It speaks directly to the pain of fragmented data. The objection handlers tucked directly under the CTA ("No credit card. No IT lift. Live in 2 weeks") act as a powerful friction-reducer right at the point of action.
*   **The #1 Fix:** The secondary CTA ("See a sample brief") is a text link that blends in. A GM who is interested but not ready to talk to sales will skip the primary CTA. Make "See a sample brief" a ghost button to capture the researcher-intent segment. 

**2. Value Pillars & "Deep Dives" (The Mechanism)**
*   **The Job:** Prove *how* it works and agitate the pain of the status quo (dashboards). 
*   **Is it doing it?** Phenomenally well. "Most software tells you what happened. Swoop tells you what to do next." sets up the paradigm shift. The copy "Dashboards don't save memberships... Swoop drafts the exact callback script" is a direct application of reducing Cognitive Load. You are selling the *result* of the data, not the data itself.
*   **The #1 Fix:** The mock UI elements (e.g., "$42.2K protected across 8 actions") are great, but the text inside them is very small. In a mobile or quick-scroll environment, visitors won't read it. Ensure the most critical number in those visuals has the highest contrast and size hierarchy.

**3. Integrations (Objection Handling)**
*   **The Job:** Answer the technical buyer's immediate objection: "Will this break my 15-year-old legacy Jonas system?"
*   **Is it doing it?** Yes. The copy "Your tools store the data. Swoop tells you exactly how to act on it." neutralizes the threat of a rip-and-replace. "Typical launch: 10 business days" and "Read-only API" eliminate the implementation fear.
*   **The #1 Fix:** While I know logo constraints exist, the text grid of integrations (Tee Sheet & Booking, Member CRM, etc.) is a bit dense. Add a simple search bar or a dropdown here saying "Check if your system is supported" that defaults to "Yes, we integrate with [Input]" to drive micro-engagement.

**4. Pricing (The Close)**
*   **The Job:** Anchor value, present the offer, and trigger the final commitment (Cialdini's Commitment/Consistency).
*   **Is it doing it?** Yes. "Start at zero. Upgrade when the math shows up" is one of the best risk-reversal headers I've seen in B2B SaaS. It triggers loss aversion in the right way. Highlighting the middle tier ($499/mo) with the orange border guides the eye perfectly.
*   **The #1 Fix:** The CTA on the free tier is "Book a Setup Call" and the middle tier is "Book a 30-Min Walkthrough". A free tier shouldn't require a "Walkthrough." If it's truly a product-led "Start at zero" motion, the CTA should be "Start for Free" or "Get Your First Brief." If it requires sales, "Book a Setup Call" feels like work. Change it to "Claim Your Free Setup".

## Revenue Impact Estimate

Here are the top 5 fixes ranked by estimated conversion impact and implementation effort. Quick wins first.

**1. Elevate the Secondary CTA (Micro-conversion)**
*   **Impact:** HIGH — You are currently losing the "interested but introverted" GM who wants to see the product but refuses to talk to sales yet.
*   **Effort:** LOW — Style the "See a sample brief" link in the hero as a high-contrast ghost button. Gate it behind a simple email capture to build your lead nurture list.

**2. Optimize CTA Copy for Outcomes**
*   **Impact:** MEDIUM — "Book a Walkthrough" is a request for the user's time (high friction). 
*   **Effort:** LOW — Change the primary hero and footer CTAs to "See Swoop in Action" or "Get Your Club's 6AM Brief". Focus on what they *get*, not what they *do*.

**3. Rename the Free Tier CTA**
*   **Impact:** MEDIUM — "Book a Setup Call" implies work. 
*   **Effort:** LOW — Change the $0 tier CTA to "Claim Your Free Setup" or "Activate Free Signals". Use language that implies ownership and ease.

**4. Add Specific ROI Anchors in the Pricing Tier**
*   **Impact:** MEDIUM — $499/mo is a specific cost. Offset it with a specific potential gain right in the card.
*   **Effort:** LOW — In the $499 "Signals + Actions" card, add a sub-bullet under the price: *(Typical ROI: 1 saved membership covers 12 months)*. This operationalizes the "math shows up" headline directly at the point of click.

**5. Make the "Rollout Timeline" Stand Out More**
*   **Impact:** LOW/MEDIUM — Implementation fear is the #1 killer of B2B SaaS deals.
*   **Effort:** LOW — The "Typical launch: 10 business days" box is currently visually subordinate to the integrations grid. Give that box an orange border or a slightly lighter background to guarantee the eye catches it on the scroll down.
