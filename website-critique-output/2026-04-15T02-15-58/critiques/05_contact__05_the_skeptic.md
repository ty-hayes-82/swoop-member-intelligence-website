## Trust Killers (What's Hurting)

*   **The "SOC 2 Type II" Claim (Severity: High):** To a sophisticated IT buyer or compliance officer, claiming SOC 2 Type II at the pre-revenue/pilot stage is a massive red flag. Type II requires a continuous audit period (usually 3–12 months) of operational controls with live data. A startup might have SOC 2 *Type I* (a point-in-time design audit), but claiming Type II prematurely implies either a fundamental misunderstanding of compliance or active misrepresentation.
*   **Future Copyright Date (Severity: Low but annoying):** The footer says "© 2026 Swoop Golf, Inc." While likely a template placeholder error, to a skeptical buyer, easily avoidable typos on a security-focused page chip away at the "attention to detail" narrative.
