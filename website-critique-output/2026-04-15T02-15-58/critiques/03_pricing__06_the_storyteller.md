## The Rewrite List

**1. The "Instant" Contradiction**
*   **Current Text:** "Book a Setup Call →" (followed immediately by) "Instant access — connects to your systems in minutes."
*   **Why it fails:** If access is instant and connects in minutes, making the user book a "setup call" introduces unnecessary friction and contradicts the premise of self-serve speed. 
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** Change the button to: **"Connect Your Systems Now →"**

**2. The Clunky Tier 3 Description**
*   **Current Text:** "Adds the Swoop member app — GPS + what members actually do on property, plus push notifications and attribution from signal to save."
*   **Why it fails:** It’s a run-on sentence that stumbles at the end. "Attribution from signal to save" slips into SaaS marketing jargon, breaking the otherwise excellent GM-focused tone.
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** "Everything above, plus the Swoop Member App. Track on-property behavior via GPS, send push notifications, and trace every saved membership exactly back to the alert that triggered it."

**3. The Redundant Spreadsheet Phrase**
*   **Current Text:** "...so your team acts instead of sorting spreadsheets. Stop sorting spreadsheets. Start saving dues."
*   **Why it fails:** The punchy staccato rhythm is great, but repeating "sorting spreadsheets" back-to-back in the same breath feels like a drafting oversight rather than intentional repetition.
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** "...so your team takes action instead of analyzing data. Stop sorting spreadsheets. Start saving dues."

**4. The Timeline Disconnect in the Footer**
*   **Current Text:** "Setup takes 15 minutes. Your first member brief arrives tomorrow morning." followed by the button **"Book a 30-min Walkthrough →"**
*   **Why it fails:** You just told them setup takes 15 minutes, but the button asks them for 30 minutes of their time just for a walkthrough. Align the CTA with the promise of speed.
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** Change the button to: **"Start Your 15-Minute Setup →"**

**5. The Confusing Micro-Copy Link**
*   **Current Text:** (Very bottom right footer) "Talk to a GM who's using it -- Book a Walkthrough"
*   **Why it fails:** This conflates two completely different high-value actions. Booking a walkthrough with sales is very different from doing a reference call with a peer GM. 
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** Separate these into distinct offers: **"Want to talk to a peer GM? Get an Intro →"** (If you don't actually offer peer intros, remove the first half entirely).

## The Missing Story
**The 3-Step Implementation Reality.** 
The copy aggressively promises "Zero IT required," "Live in 14 days," and "Setup takes 15 minutes." For a GM scarred by months-long club software deployments, this sounds almost too good to be true. What is missing is a tiny, highly visual 1-2-3 step micro-story showing *exactly* how that 15-minute setup happens. (e.g., *1. Authorize read-only API -> 2. Swoop models your last 3 years of data overnight -> 3. Wake up to 5 at-risk member alerts in your inbox.*) Demystifying the "how" will completely neutralize their final lingering objection.
