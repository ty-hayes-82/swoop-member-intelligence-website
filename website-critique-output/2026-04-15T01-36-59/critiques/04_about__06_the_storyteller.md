## The Rewrite List

**1. The Cliché Team Intro**
*   **Current Text:** "An extension of your team, not a disruption to it."
*   **Why it fails:** "An extension of your team" is one of the most overused clichés in B2B SaaS. It dilutes the very real, very impressive subtext below it about sitting in their board decks.
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** "We sit in your data so you can sit with your members."

**2. The Generic Proof Eyebrow**
*   **Current Text:** "Intelligence in action."
*   **Why it fails:** It sounds like a placeholder headline from a template. It doesn't tee up the incredibly strong, specific metrics that follow. 
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** "What happens when you turn Swoop on." (Or: "The exact ROI of an intelligent club.")

**3. The Uninspired Testimonial Label**
*   **Current Text:** "WHAT GMS ARE SAYING / From founding-partner GMs."
*   **Why it fails:** "What X are saying" is invisible to the B2B buyer's brain. You have fantastic, reputation-risking quotes here ("first club-tech vendor deliverable I've ever forwarded to my board"). The headline should match that weight.
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** "THE BOARD-LEVEL IMPACT / GMs who finally ditched their spreadsheets."

**4. The Weak FAQ Intro**
*   **Current Text:** "Things GMs ask us."
*   **Why it fails:** It's a bit too casual and passive for a section that successfully handles serious objections (data ownership, 3-year Jonas contracts).
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** "Tough questions from skeptical GMs."

**5. The Missing Urgency in the Moat**
*   **Current Text:** "Why this is hard to copy. / 46 operational integrations already wired and tested..."
*   **Why it fails:** "Why this is hard to copy" speaks to *your* business model, not the GM's benefit. Frame your moat as their shortcut.
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** "We did the messy integration work so you don't have to."

Right now, the copy heavily anchors on the GM's operational relief (spreadsheets, 10 AM delays). What is missing in the middle of the page is a specific "Day-in-the-Life" scenario showing exactly how this tool manifests in the real world.
