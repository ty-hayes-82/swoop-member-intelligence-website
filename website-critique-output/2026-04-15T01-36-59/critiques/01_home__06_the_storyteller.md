**1. The Overused Cliché**
*   **Current Text:** "Now you have a team that never sleeps."
*   **Why it fails:** It's one of the oldest B2B tech clichés in the book. It sounds like a generic SaaS pitch, which breaks the highly customized, industry-specific spell the rest of the copy has cast.
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** "Four dedicated specialists. Zero added headcount."

**2. The Academic Integration Jargon**
*   **Current Text:** "Cross-System Behavioral Correlation. How a member's dining patterns predict their tee sheet behavior. How staffing gaps correlate with revenue drops."
*   **Why it fails:** "Cross-System Behavioral Correlation" sounds like a university research paper, not a tool for a golf club GM. 
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** "Connect the Dots Across Departments. See how a member's dining habits predict their tee times, or how a staffing shortage in the Grill Room impacts pro shop revenue."

**5. The Rollout Subtext**

## The Missing Story
**The GM's "Night Before the Board Meeting" Before & After.**
The site does a phenomenal job telling the *Member's* story (James pulling into the parking lot, the 100th round milestone). What is missing is a brief, vivid depiction of the GM's pain. 

A quick "Before Swoop vs. After Swoop" scenario focusing on a specific, painful operational moment would add massive weight. For example: Contrast the GM spending four hours on a Thursday night exporting CSVs from Jonas, the POS, and the tee sheet to justify a staffing increase to the board, versus the GM clicking one button on Friday morning to print a Swoop "Board-Ready Attribution" report. You hint at it beautifully ("Not a feeling"), but painting the exact "before" picture would make the solution irresistible.
