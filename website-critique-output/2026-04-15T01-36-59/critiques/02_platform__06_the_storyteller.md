## The Rewrite List

**1. The Banned Cliché in the Subtext**
- **Current text:** "Plans scale seamlessly. Starting at $499/mo." (Located under the main hero CTA)
- **Why it fails:** "Seamlessly" is a lazy, overused tech cliché. In a site packed with concrete, specific language, this word sticks out as generic filler. 
- **Suggested rewrite:** "Plans scale with your membership size. Starting at $499/mo."

**2. The Slightly Threatening Integration Headline**
- **Current text:** "Your tools store the data. Swoop decides what to do with it." (Integrations section)
- **Why it fails:** While punchy, "Swoop decides" triggers a loss-of-control anxiety for a GM relying on AI. You do a great job handling this objection elsewhere ("GM Approval Required"), but the headline shouldn't create the fear in the first place.
- **Suggested rewrite:** "Your tools store the data. Swoop tells you exactly how to act on it."

**3. The Generic Marketing Speak in the Agent Descriptions**
- **Current text:** "Engagement Autopilot: Monitors declining participation and proposes targeted outreach for member reactivation."
- **Why it fails:** "Targeted outreach for member reactivation" is sterile marketing terminology. It lacks the visceral punch of your other agent descriptions (like "Surfaces unresolved complaints").
- **Suggested rewrite:** "Engagement Autopilot: Spots members who haven’t visited in 30 days and drafts a personal invite to get them back."

**4. The Clunky Mid-Page CTA**
- **Current text:** "See these six capabilities with your club’s data →"
- **Why it fails:** "Capabilities" is a dry, corporate word. It interrupts the narrative of having active "agents" working for you. 
- **Suggested rewrite:** "See what these agents can find in your club’s data →"

**5. The Underwhelming Footer Headline**
- **Current text:** "Ready to change how you run your club?"
- **Why it fails:** It’s a standard B2B wrap-up question. The page starts with a massive, specific hook ("Stop guessing who's drifting"); it needs to end with the same level of specific, outcome-driven energy.
- **Suggested rewrite:** "Stop guessing. Start protecting your club's revenue today."

## The Missing Story
**The "Before & After" Board Meeting Narrative.** 
You have a brilliant hook with the "Karen Wittman" micro-story ("Take a dollar number to the board. Not a feeling."). What would elevate this even further is a stylized, Day-in-the-Life contrast of the GM's ultimate proving ground: the monthly board meeting. 

Show the "Before Swoop" reality (shuffling Excel sheets, defensive posturing about why F&B revenue is down, subjective guesses about member happiness) versus the "After Swoop" reality (walking in with a single printed page, pointing to exactly how many dues were saved that month, and leading with confidence). You are selling software, but you are *marketing* job security and professional pride. Make that contrast undeniable.
