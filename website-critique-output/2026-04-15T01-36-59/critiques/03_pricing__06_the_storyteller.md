## The Rewrite List

Even on a near-perfect page, there is room for slight optimization, specifically around harmonizing setup timelines and sharpening microcopy.

**1. The Setup Time Dissonance**
*   **Current Text:** In the $0/mo tier: *"Instant access — connects to your systems in minutes."* vs. the bottom trust strip: *"Most clubs are live in under 2 weeks."*
*   **Why it fails:** GMs hate IT surprises. If the copy says "minutes" in one place and "2 weeks" in another, it creates a subtle trust fracture. You need to clarify the difference between technical connection and operational readiness.
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** Change the bottom strip to: *"Systems connect in minutes. First member intelligence brief arrives in under 2 weeks."*

**2. The Calculator Secondary CTA**
*   **Current Text:** *"Not ready to book? Email this ROI report to your board →"*
*   **Why it fails:** It's a fantastic idea, but "Email this ROI report" sounds like a generic system action. Frame it as a tool for the GM to look smart and proactive to their board of directors.
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** *"Not ready to book? Send these projected savings to your Board →"*

**3. The Calculator Sub-Headline**
*   **Current Text:** *"Adjust the sliders to see your club’s exposure — and what Swoop recovers."*
*   **Why it fails:** It's good, but slightly passive. The calculator is the most powerful emotional tool on the page; the copy should punch harder to drive interaction.
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** *"Enter your club's numbers to see exactly what silent attrition is costing you today."*

**4. The Value Reinforcement in the Middle Tier**
*   **Current Text:** *"Signals + Actions $499/mo ... Everything in Signals, plus Swoop drafts the callback, comp offer, and staffing shift in plain English — so your team acts instead of sorting spreadsheets."*
*   **Why it fails:** The copy here is excellent, but the transition from $0 to $499 needs a slightly stronger financial anchor so $499 feels like a steal compared to the money saved.
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** *"Everything in Signals, plus Swoop drafts the exact callback scripts and comp offers needed to save the member. Stop sorting spreadsheets; start saving dues."*

**5. The FAQ Header**
*   **Current Text:** *"Things GMs ask us."*
*   **Why it fails:** It actually doesn't fail—it's great. However, it's floating a bit without a subheadline to transition into the technical questions.
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** Keep the header, but add a subheadline: *"You're putting your reputation on the line. We don't take that lightly. Here's how we protect your data and your time."*

## The Missing Story

**The "Hero GM" Scenario.** 
The financial and logical story here is ironclad, but B2B buying is also deeply emotional. A GM's biggest fear isn't just losing money; it's being caught off guard at a board meeting when a legacy family quits unexpectedly. 

What’s missing is a 2-3 sentence "Day-in-the-Life" narrative block—perhaps placed right before the FAQ—that paints the picture of the GM walking into a board meeting armed with Swoop's data. 

*Narrative concept:* "Imagine sitting down for your Friday management meeting. Instead of guessing why F&B revenue is down, you hand your team a list of 5 at-risk families and the exact scripts to bring them back this weekend. That's the power of Club Intelligence." 

Showing the GM *feeling* in control, rather than just *being* in control, will push this already stellar page to perfection.
