## The Rewrite List

**1. The Timeline Contradiction**
*   **Current:** "See what Swoop would find at your club — in 15 minutes." (Directly above "30 minutes. Your real numbers.")
*   **Why it fails:** Cognitive friction. The hero section promises a 30-minute walkthrough. The paragraph below says 30 minutes. The headline suddenly says 15 minutes. Confused buyers don't fill out forms.
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** "See what Swoop can uncover in your tee sheet — in exactly 30 minutes."

**2. The SaaS Cliché**
*   **Current:** "Seamless read-only API connection to Jonas, Clubessential, Northstar, etc. Zero IT required."
*   **Why it fails:** "Seamless" is a lazy, invisible word in B2B tech. GMs are terrified of software integrations because they are historically painful. Be specific about *why* it's easy.
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** "Instant, read-only API connection to Jonas, Clubessential, Northstar, etc. Zero IT required."

**3. The Academic Subheadline**
*   **Current:** "Book a live walkthrough with your own operating scenarios: tee sheet leakage, at-risk members, F&B staffing pressure, and revenue pipeline blind spots."
*   **Why it fails:** "Operating scenarios" sounds a bit like an MBA textbook. You want to focus on the pain points they actually experience on the floor. 
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** "Book a live walkthrough to uncover your club's hidden blind spots: tee sheet leakage, quietly churning members, and missed F&B revenue."

**4. The Presumptive Data Connection**
*   **Current:** "We connect to your tee sheet before the call so you see exactly what Swoop surfaces for a club like yours."
*   **Why it fails:** GMs are highly protective of member data. Stating "We connect to your tee sheet before the call" based simply on them submitting a Name/Email form feels presumptuous and alarming. They need to know *they* authorize it first.
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** "Once you book, we'll guide you through a secure, 5-minute data connection so we can review your real numbers on the call."

**5. The Redundant Microcopy**
*   **Current:** "No credit card • 30 minutes • Your club's own data" (Located beneath the "What happens next" box)
*   **Why it fails:** It repeats the 30-minute timeframe that was just stated in step 3 directly above it. Use this premium space near the email link to reinforce risk reversal.

## The Missing Story
**The "After the Data" Human Element.** 
The page is brilliantly focused on the *mechanics* of data extraction and identifying the problem. But Club management is fundamentally a hospitality business. What is missing is a brief sentence or concept illustrating what the GM actually *does* with this intelligence. 

The testimonial touches on it beautifully ("One dinner comp and a follow-up call..."). The main copy should echo this transition from *data* to *hospitality*. For example, framing the ultimate benefit not just as "a prioritized action list," but as: *"Stop guessing who is leaving, and start having the right conversations on the 9th green."* Connect the digital insights back to the analog, human reality of running a private club.
