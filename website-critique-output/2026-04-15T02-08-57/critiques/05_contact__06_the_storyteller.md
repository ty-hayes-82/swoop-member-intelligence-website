## The Rewrite List

**1. The Repetitive Time-Commitment Hook**
*   **Current:** "See what Swoop would find at your club — in 30 minutes." (Dark section headline)
*   **Why it fails:** You've already established the 30-minute timeframe in the hero section, the sub-headline, and the button. Saying it again here creates fatigue. Use this prime real estate to pivot from *time* to *revelation*.
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** "See your club's hidden revenue leaks. Using your own data."

**2. The Cliché in the Fine Print**
*   **Current:** "Seamless read-only API connection to Jonas, Clubessential, Northstar, etc."
*   **Why it fails:** "Seamless" is a lazy, overused SaaS cliché. GMs don't believe software is ever "seamless." They want to know it's secure, direct, and requires no effort from them. 
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** "Zero-config, read-only API connection to Jonas, Clubessential, Northstar, etc."

**3. The Redundant Form Introduction**
*   **Current:** "30 minutes. Your real numbers. We connect to your tee sheet before the call so you see exactly what Swoop surfaces for a club like yours."
*   **Why it fails:** "For a club like yours" implies you are showing them a generic lookalike dashboard, contradicting the previous sentence ("Your real numbers"). 
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** "We analyze a secure snapshot of your tee sheet before we meet, so we spend our time fixing your active revenue leaks—not guessing where they are."

**4. The Passive Scenario List**
*   **Current:** "Book a live walkthrough with your own operating scenarios: tee sheet leakage, at-risk members, F&B staffing pressure, and revenue pipeline blind spots."
*   **Why it fails:** It reads like a list of features disguised as scenarios. Make the GM the active participant in this walkthrough.
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** "Bring your toughest operational blind spots to the call. We'll map your tee sheet leakage, flag at-risk members, and pinpoint F&B gaps on the spot."

**5. The Relatability of the Placeholder Text**

## The Missing Story
**The Cost of Inaction (The "While You Wait" Narrative)**
The page expertly outlines what the GM *gets* by booking a demo, but it misses the opportunity to agitate the status quo. What is happening right now while the GM delays? A simple line injecting urgency based on the silent nature of member disengagement would elevate the conversion pressure. 

*Example injection:* "Every month you wait, members are quietly skipping their usual Thursday dinners. We catch the behavior before it becomes a resignation letter."
