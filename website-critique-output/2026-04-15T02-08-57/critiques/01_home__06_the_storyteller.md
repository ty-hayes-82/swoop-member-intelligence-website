## The Rewrite List

**1. The Perspective Slip**
*   **Current Text:** "What your GM sees at 6:15 AM." (Section heading above the dashboard UI)
*   **Why it fails:** The entire website addresses the GM directly ("*Your* tee sheet," "Now *you* have a team"). Shifting to "your GM" implies you are suddenly talking to the Club Owner or the Board, which breaks the established connection with the primary reader.
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** "What you see at 6:15 AM." or "Your new 6:15 AM reality."

**2. Diluted Punchlines (Repetition)**
*   **Current Text:** "Board-ready attribution. Not a feeling." (Hero trio) AND "Take a dollar number to the board. Not a feeling." (Section trio)
*   **Why it fails:** "Not a feeling" is a fantastic, punchy fragment that contrasts perfectly with data. But using it twice on the same page makes it feel like a copy-paste error rather than a deliberate motif.
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** Keep the first one. Change the second one to: "Take a dollar number to the board. Leave the guesswork behind."

**3. The Academic Jargon Shift**
*   **Current Text:** "Real-Time Location Intelligence" and "Cross-System Behavioral Correlation" (Integrations section)
*   **Why it fails:** Up to this point, the copy has been grounded in the gritty reality of country clubs. Suddenly, the subheads sound like a whitepaper from an enterprise data warehouse. It breaks the conversational tone.
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** Change to "On-Property Member Tracking" and "Connect Behavior to Revenue."

**4. The Weak Footer Headline**
*   **Current Text:** "See what your club misses today."
*   **Why it fails:** The phrasing is slightly awkward ("misses" vs. "is missing"), and it doesn't pack the same punch as the rest of the site's copy. It feels like an afterthought before the final CTA.
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** "Find the revenue hiding in your club." or "See what your systems are missing."

**5. The Integration Subheadline**
*   **Current Text:** "Your tools store the data. Swoop tells you exactly how to act on it."
*   **Why it fails:** It's good, but "how to act on it" is a bit wordy and slightly passive compared to the aggressive, action-oriented tone of the hero section. 
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** "Your tools hoard data. Swoop turns it into action."

## The Missing Story
**The Staff Buy-In Narrative.** 
The copy speaks brilliantly to the GM, the Board, and the Member. But it leaves out a critical stakeholder: the club staff (the Head Pro, the F&B Director, the Starter). If an AI is suddenly dictating "who gets a free burger" or "who to call," staff might feel threatened or micromanaged. A brief, one-paragraph story acknowledging the staff's relief—showing how Swoop empowers the front line to be hospitality heroes without the administrative burden—would eliminate the GM's unspoken fear of staff pushback during implementation.
