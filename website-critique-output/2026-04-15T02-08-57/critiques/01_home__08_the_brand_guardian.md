## On-Brand Elements
* **Typography System Execution:** The page perfectly balances the three specified typefaces. Plus Jakarta Sans handles the body/UI flawlessly, Fraunces brings editorial weight to headlines ("Your tee sheet...", "One screen."), and JetBrains Mono is beautifully utilized for operational data points (`19:08:52`, `$8,400`, `10 business days.`) and terminal-style callouts (`> $18K dues at risk.`).
* **Dark Section Palette:** The hero, "Team that never sleeps", and integration sections strictly use the brand's dark charcoal (#1B1814 / #141210) rather than standard black or gray. The brass (#B5956A) is elegantly used for eyebrows like "YOU HAVE A 300-MEMBER CLUB...".
* **Tone and Voice:** The copy perfectly encapsulates the operationally specific, direct tone. Phrases like "Take a dollar number to the board. Not a feeling." and "The model gets smarter." speak directly to GM pain points without startup fluff.
* **Outcome-Specific CTAs:** Avoids generic language completely. "Book a Walkthrough", "See a sample brief", "Book a Setup Call", and "Get a sample morning briefing →" are highly on-brand.
* **Pricing Terminology:** Successfully uses the mandatory "FOUNDING-PARTNER PICK" eyebrow in the pricing tier instead of generic SaaS terms like "Beta" or "Most Popular".

## Off-Brand Elements
* **Generic Success/Status Colors:** 
  * *What it is:* A bright generic green (`#10B981` or similar) is used in the UI mockups for the "APPROVED" pill and the "LIVE - 6 AGENTS ONLINE" status dot.
  * *What it should be:* The Swoop semantic scale (amber/orange-deep) should be used for status indicators, avoiding generic green/red completely.
  * *Where it appears:* The Z-pattern feature mockups ("Before the first tee time" and "What your GM sees at 6:15 AM").
* **Inconsistent CTA Arrow Syntax:**
  * *What it is:* Some buttons use a right arrow suffix ("Book the 30-Minute Walkthrough →", "Book a Setup Call →"), while the primary hero and nav buttons do not ("Book a Walkthrough").
  * *What it should be:* A unified syntax for primary and secondary CTA buttons across the design system.
  * *Where it appears:* Nav/Hero vs. mid-page and Pricing section.
* **Inconsistent Quote Formatting:**
  * *What it is:* The pull quote in the hero uses standard quotation marks ("Recovered $74K..."), while the mid-page quote ("James doesn't know Swoop exists...") lacks quotation marks but uses italics, and the data card quote ("Swoop flagged...") uses italics without marks. 
  * *What it should be:* A single consistent typographical treatment for club/member pull quotes.
  * *Where it appears:* Hero section, "Prove It" data card, and Member Experience section.

## Brand Consistency vs. Other Pages
* **Background Neutrals:** The transition from cream (#F7F5F2) in the Z-pattern to white (#FFFFFF) in the pricing and footer is handled well, but the central pricing card utilizes the sand/cream background to pop against the white—a great component pattern that should be standardized for highlighted tiers across the site.
* **Button Styles:** The hero secondary button is a transparent text button ("See a sample brief"), whereas the pricing secondary buttons are outlined cards ("Book a Setup Call →"). Ensure this is an intentional light-mode vs. dark-mode system variation and not component drift.
