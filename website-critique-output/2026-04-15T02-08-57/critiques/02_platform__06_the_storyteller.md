## The Rewrite List

**1. The "Upside-Down" Hero Copy**
*   **Current text:** "One dashboard shows which members are drifting, why, and what to do next — assembled from your existing systems overnight." (Located in the tiny eyebrow text above the H1).
*   **Why it fails:** This is your actual sub-headline, but it's trapped in a tiny "kicker" font above the main headline. It forces the reader to read out of order (small text, then big text, then there is no subtext below the big text to ground it).
*   **Suggested rewrite:**

    *   *H1:* Stop guessing who's drifting. Start protecting your dues.
    *   *Subheadline (below H1):* One dashboard shows you exactly who is at risk, why they're leaving, and what to do next — assembled from your existing systems overnight.

*   **Why it fails:** Telling the user "this panel auto-cycles" breaks the fourth wall. You're talking about how the website works, which distracts from how the *product* works. Sell the invisible labor, not the web animation.

## The Missing Story
**The Empathy Origin (The "We Lived It" Element)**
The copy is so deeply fluent in the daily miseries of running a private club (stitching together Jonas CRM data, manual tee sheet backfills, facing the board) that it begs the question: *Who built this?* 

Because there are no testimonials yet (understood as a pre-revenue constraint), the trust must come from the founders' domain expertise. Adding a short "Founder's Note" or a "Built by operators who felt your pain" section would bridge the trust gap. When you name-drop legacy systems like Jonas and call out specific pain points, the buyer wants to know you've sat in their chair. A brief origin story validating *why* you decided to solve this specific problem would elevate the site from a great software pitch to a trusted industry partner.
