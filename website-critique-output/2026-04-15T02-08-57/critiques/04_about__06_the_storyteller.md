## The Rewrite List

**1. The "Company-Centric" Subhead**
*   **Current:** "MOAT: Why this is hard to copy."
*   **Why it fails:** "Moat" is VC/founder terminology, not buyer terminology. A Club GM doesn't care if your software is hard for another startup to copy; they care that it works reliably with their legacy systems. 
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** "THE INTEGRATION ENGINE: Built for the legacy systems others won't touch."

**2. The Slightly Invasive Team Description**
*   **Current:** "6 founding-partner clubs — for six months we sit in your systems, on your calls, and in your board deck."
*   **Why it fails:** While the intent is to show deep partnership and "concierge" service, "sitting in your systems and on your calls" can sound a bit invasive or disruptive, directly contradicting the headline above it ("not a disruption to it").
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** "We are actively embedded with 6 founding clubs—optimizing their workflows, supporting their team, and arming them for board meetings."

**3. The Wordy Cohort Pitch**
*   **Current:** "A small cohort of clubs gets hands-on onboarding, direct roadmap influence, and pricing locked for life."
*   **Why it fails:** "A small cohort" sounds slightly academic. It's not bad, but it lacks the exclusive, VIP ring that appeals to private club management.
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** "An exclusive group of partner clubs receiving concierge onboarding, direct input on our product roadmap, and pricing locked in forever."

**4. The Final CTA Sub-bullets**
*   **Current:** "2. We pull a sample brief from your tee sheet and POS (5-min secure read) Seamless read-only API connection to Jonas, Clubessential, Northstar, etc. Zero IT required."
*   **Why it fails:** It's a bit of a run-on thought masquerading as a bullet point. The parentheticals interrupt the reading flow right at the point of conversion.
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** "2. We run a secure, 5-minute data sync to generate your sample brief. (Works seamlessly via read-only APIs with Jonas, Clubessential, and Northstar—zero IT required)."

**5. The Early Warning System Metric**
*   **Current:** "6 days / Average advance notice on at-risk members / Detected James Whitfield resignation risk 6 days before it happened by connecting POS spend decline, CRM complaint, and tee sheet pattern changes."
*   **Why it fails:** The metric is great, but the story underneath is the real gold. The phrasing "Detected James Whitfield resignation risk 6 days before it happened" is a bit robotic.
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** "6 days / Advance notice on at-risk members / Swoop flagged a member's resignation risk 6 days *before* he quit—simply by correlating a drop in POS spend, a CRM complaint, and a missed tee time."
