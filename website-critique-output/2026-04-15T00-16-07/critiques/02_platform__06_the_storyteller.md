## The Rewrite List

### 1. Hero Subheadline

**Current:** *"The member intelligence platform for private clubs. It works overnight to surface who's disengaging and what to do about it — so your team can act before it's too late."*

**Why it fails:** It's functional but bland after such a strong headline. "Member intelligence platform" is category labeling, not story-building. "So your team can act before it's too late" is vague urgency — too late for what, exactly?

**Rewrite:** *"Every night, Swoop reads your club's data — POS, tee sheets, dining, events — and tells your team exactly which members need attention and what to say. Before a resignation letter ever gets written."*

## The Missing Story

**The absent element: A single, specific club story told in the GM's voice.**

This page does nearly everything right architecturally, but it operates entirely in the hypothetical. There is no named club, no quoted GM, no "before Swoop / after Swoop" narrative. For a product that claims to protect dues revenue and catch disengagement, the most powerful proof would be something like:

> *"At Pinetree Country Club, GM Sarah Chen was losing 8-12 members a quarter and didn't know why until the resignation letters arrived. Within 60 days of using Swoop, her team intercepted 6 members who had stopped booking tee times and hadn't dined in 45+ days. Three personal calls later, all six renewed. That's $47,000 in dues that would have walked out the door."*

This doesn't need to be a full case study page — it needs to be **one section on this page**, between the feature demonstration and the final CTA. Right now, the page says "trust us, this works." A single story section would say "here's proof it works, and here's a person whose career got easier because of it."

The page also lacks any **day-in-the-life contrast** — a "Monday morning without Swoop" vs. "Monday morning with Swoop" framing that would make the operational relief tangible. The "daily brief, written overnight" section hints at this but doesn't commit to showing the GM's actual experience change. Show me the GM who used to spend Tuesday mornings pulling reports now spending that time on the floor talking to members. That's the story that closes deals.
