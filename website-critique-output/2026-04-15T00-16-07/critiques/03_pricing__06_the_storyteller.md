## Narrative Verdict

This pricing page is doing something genuinely rare and smart: it leads with ROI before it leads with price. The "Recover your software costs in 60 days. Start for zero." headline combined with the interactive ROI calculator creates a narrative that reframes the entire pricing conversation from "what does it cost?" to "what am I losing by not doing this?" However, the page stumbles in the middle tiers section where the copy becomes feature-listy and loses the emotional momentum it built at the top. The single most impactful copy change would be rewriting the tier names and descriptions to mirror the buyer's journey ("See the problem → Fix the problem → Run on autopilot") rather than using generic SaaS packaging language.

## The Rewrite List

### 1. Tier Name: "Signals"

**Current text:** "Signals — $0/mo — Ready-only alerts. Surveillance your member data and factual attrition risk. Low effort, and start seeing at-risk members."

**Why it fails:** "Signals" is abstract product jargon. "Surveillance your member data" is awkward phrasing and slightly creepy. "Low effort, and start seeing at-risk members" is grammatically clunky and doesn't tell the GM what changes for them tomorrow.

**Suggested rewrite:**
> **Watch Mode — $0/mo**
> See which members are pulling away — before they resign. Connect your club's systems in minutes and get weekly at-risk alerts delivered to your inbox. No cost, no commitment, no surprises.

### 2. Tier Name: "Signals + Actions"

**Current text:** "Signals + Actions — $499/mo — Everything in Signals, plus Swoop does the outreach, sends the emails and texts to re-engage — so your marketing team doesn't have to start from zero or learning new tools and new processes of outreach."

**Why it fails:** This is a 40-word run-on sentence that buries the value. The real promise — "we do the outreach for you" — gets lost in a clause about "learning new tools and new processes of outreach." The description is defensive rather than aspirational.

**Suggested rewrite:**
> **Signals + Actions — $499/mo**
> We spot at-risk members. Then we re-engage them for you — personalized emails and texts, sent automatically, using proven playbooks. Your team doesn't lift a finger. Your board sees the saves.

### 3. CTA Button: "Calculate your ROI"

**Current text:** "Calculate your ROI"

**Why it fails:** It's not bad, but it's generic fintech/SaaS language. On a page that already establishes the "dues revenue walking out your door" metaphor, this CTA misses an opportunity to continue that narrative thread.

**Suggested rewrite:**
> "See What You're Losing"

### 4. Pricing Tier Bullet: "AI-powered member wellness scores"

**Current text:** "AI-powered member wellness scores"

**Why it fails:** This is a feature masquerading as a benefit. What does a "wellness score" actually mean for the GM reading this? It uses the "AI-powered" modifier as a crutch — the kind of phrase that makes every B2B SaaS product sound identical. A GM doesn't buy "wellness scores." They buy knowing which members need attention this week.

**Suggested rewrite:**
> "Every member gets a risk score updated weekly — so you know exactly who needs a personal call before they resign"

### 5. FAQ Question: "Do I need to replace my current software?"

**Current text:** "No. Swoop sits on top of your existing systems and connects via API. We support 25 integrations across 12 categories."

**Why it fails:** The answer is technically correct but emotionally flat. This is the #1 objection a GM has — "Is this going to be another painful implementation that my staff hates?" — and the answer doesn't address the emotional weight of that fear. "25 integrations across 12 categories" is a stat that means nothing to a non-technical buyer.

**Suggested rewrite:**
> "No — and your team won't need to learn anything new. Swoop connects to the systems you already use (Jonas, Northstar, Club Essentials, ForeTees, and 20+ others). Most clubs are fully connected in under a week. Your staff won't even notice we're there."

## The Missing Story

**What's absent: A single, named club's pricing journey told as a mini-narrative.**

The ROI calculator is smart, but it's hypothetical — it shows what *could* happen. What this page desperately needs, placed between the calculator and the pricing tiers, is a concrete before/after story:

> *"Pinetree Country Club started on Watch Mode in January 2024 — free. Within three weeks, Swoop flagged 23 members showing early disengagement signals. Their GM, Sarah, upgraded to Signals + Actions. Swoop's automated outreach re-engaged 14 of those members. By March, Sarah moved to the full platform. This year, Pinetree's attrition dropped from 8.2% to 4.1% — saving $312,000 in dues revenue. The board asked how she did it."*

This kind of narrative does three things the current page cannot:
1. **It makes the tier progression feel natural** — not like upselling, but like a GM's logical journey from skepticism to confidence
2. **It gives the $499 and $1,499 price tags an emotional anchor** — $499/mo feels trivial next to "$312,000 saved"
3. **It answers the unspoken question every GM has on a pricing page:** "Has someone like me actually done this and not regretted it?"

The page builds conviction through logic (ROI calculator, stats, feature lists) but never through *identification*. A GM needs to see themselves in someone else's story before they'll book that walkthrough. Right now, the closest thing is the stat "5 of 7 founding partner clubs recovered Swoop's annual cost within 60 days" — which is strong but anonymous. Name one. Tell that story. That's the difference between a pricing page that informs and one that converts.
