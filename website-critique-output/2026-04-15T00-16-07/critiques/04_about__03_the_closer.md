## The Conversion Audit

### HOME/HERO SECTION
**Page's Job:** Communicate what Swoop does, for whom, and why it matters — in under 5 seconds. Convert high-intent visitors immediately; hook low-intent visitors into scrolling.

**Is it doing the job?** Partially. The headline **"Stop digging for answers. Get one actionable morning briefing"** is benefit-forward and punchy. But it fails the "swap test" — you could put almost any analytics tool's name on this. The subhead mentions "AI-powered member intelligence" but buries the audience (club GMs/COOs) and the category (private clubs). The orange "Book a Walkthrough" CTA button is visually dominant, which is correct.

**#1 Fix:** Rewrite the headline to include the buyer and the specific outcome: **"Club GMs: Stop digging through spreadsheets. Get a daily briefing that flags at-risk members, F&B opportunities, and operational blind spots."** This passes the 5-second test and names the persona. Add a specificity anchor like "Used by 12+ founding partner clubs" directly beneath the CTA to leverage social proof at the highest-traffic viewport (authority principle).

**Is it doing the job?** Moderately. Three team members (Tyler Hogan, Jaxson Mitchell, Alex Chen) are shown with headshots (appear as placeholder circles), titles, and short bios. The framing "An extension of your team, not a disruption to it" is good objection-handling copy (addresses the "this will be hard to implement" fear). **#1 Fix:** Replace placeholder circle avatars with actual headshots. Generic avatar icons destroy the authority principle — these need to look like real humans a GM would trust. If headshots aren't available, remove this section entirely; fake social proof is worse than no social proof.

**Page's Job:** Provide undeniable, specific proof that the product delivers results.

**Is it doing the job?** Yes — this is the strongest section on the page. The dark background creates visual contrast (Von Restorff isolation effect). The pull quote with quotation marks is prominent. The stats beneath it — **"12 GMs actively partnered"**, **"$1 reported saved monthly"** (or similar) — and the numbered proof points create specificity. However, the actual quote text and attribution are hard to fully parse from the screenshot.

**#1 Fix:** Ensure the quote is attributed to a named GM with their club name and title. "This saved us 6 hours a week" from "Tyler R., GM at Oakmont Country Club" is 10x more persuasive than an unattributed quote (authority + specificity).

### "INTELLIGENCE IN ACTION" (Metrics Section)
**Page's Job:** Quantify the value of Swoop with hard numbers that a GM can use to justify the purchase internally.

**Is it doing the job?** Very well. This is strong conversion architecture. Four metrics are displayed with large typography:
- **6 days** — Average activation time for new members
- **91%** — Percentage with retention/satisfaction
- **$312** — Average revenue per [something — likely member/month]
- **$1.38M** — At-risk [revenue/members]

Each has explanatory subtext. This section directly addresses the "prove it" objection and gives GMs ammunition for board presentations (commitment/consistency — if they engage with these numbers, they're more likely to convert).

**#1 Fix:** Add a contextual CTA directly below these metrics: **"Want to see these numbers for YOUR club? Book a walkthrough →"**. Right now, this section builds conviction but provides no conversion outlet — the visitor has to scroll further to find a button. This is a missed conversion moment (Fogg: motivation is high, but there's no trigger).

### "FOUNDING PARTNER PROGRAM" SECTION
**Page's Job:** Create an exclusive, high-commitment conversion path for early adopters with authentic scarcity.

**Is it doing the job?** Mostly. The section clearly communicates three benefits: **"Hands-on Onboarding," "Shape the Roadmap," "Locked-in Pricing."** The sub-CTA "Only X of Y founding partner spots remaining" creates legitimate scarcity (not a dark pattern — this is a real capacity constraint for an early-stage company). The orange button **"Claim a Founding Partner Spot"** is action-oriented.

**#1 Fix:** This section competes directly with "Book a Walkthrough" — and it's unclear whether clicking "Claim a Founding Partner Spot" IS the walkthrough, or a separate commitment. Clarify the relationship: make "Book a Walkthrough" the universal first step, and frame the Founding Partner Program as what you unlock DURING/AFTER the walkthrough. E.g., **"Book a walkthrough to see if your club qualifies for a founding partner spot."** This sequences the commitment ladder correctly (Cialdini's commitment/consistency: small yes → bigger yes).

### "FROM FOUNDING-PARTNER GMs" (Testimonials)
**Page's Job:** Provide named, specific social proof from peers that reduces perceived risk.

**Is it doing the job?** Yes — this is well-executed. Three testimonial cards with:
- 5-star ratings (visual shorthand)
- Specific quotes referencing outcomes (mentions of ROI, board, membership data)
- Named attributions with titles
- Orange highlight on names

The placement after the Founding Partner section is logical but suboptimal — these should appear BEFORE the conversion ask, not after.

**#1 Fix:** Move at least one testimonial ABOVE the Founding Partner CTA. Place the most outcome-specific quote (the one mentioning dollar amounts or time saved) directly above the "Claim a Founding Partner Spot" button. The persuasion sequence should be: Social Proof → CTA, not CTA → Social Proof.

Below the testimonials, there's a secondary CTA block: **"Ready to see this for your club?"** with **"Book a Walkthrough"** — this is good funnel reinforcement but comes immediately after the Founding Partner CTA, further muddying which conversion path is primary.

**#1 Fix:** The pricing question ("What does Swoop cost?") is buried in a 15+ item accordion list. This is the #1 objection for any B2B buyer. Hiding pricing increases form submissions from unqualified leads and decreases submissions from qualified ones — net negative for pipeline quality.

**Page's Job:** Capture visitors who've scrolled the entire page and are now at maximum conviction.

**Is it doing the job?** Partially. The section header **"See what your club misses today and can recover tomorrow"** is a strong loss-aversion frame (Kahneman/Tversky — losses are felt 2x more than gains). There appears to be a form with fields and a **"Book a Walkthrough"** button. A pull quote is included for final social proof reinforcement.

**#1 Fix:** This form needs to be ruthlessly minimal. Every field beyond email and club name is friction. If I see a phone number field, that's an immediate conversion killer for top-of-funnel visitors. Strip it to: Name, Club Name, Email, and a single "Book a Walkthrough" button. Add a micro-copy line below: "15-minute call. No commitment. We'll show you your club's data." (Reduces perceived risk.)

### Additional Observations (Non-Ranked)

- **No sticky header CTA:** The nav has "Book a Walkthrough" in the top-right, but on a page this long (15+ scroll-lengths), a sticky/floating CTA button on mobile and desktop would capture intent at any scroll depth. Medium impact, low effort.
- **Page is labeled "About" but functions as a hybrid About/Landing page.** This isn't inherently bad — but the URL/nav label sets an informational expectation, not a conversion expectation. Consider whether high-intent PPC traffic should land here or on a dedicated conversion page.
- **The team section avatars appear to be placeholders** (gray circles). If these are real team members with no photos, remove the section. Empty avatars actively destroy trust. If photos exist, add them immediately.
- **No video or interactive demo.** For a product that delivers a "morning briefing," showing a 60-second screen recording of what that briefing looks like would be the single highest-conviction asset possible. "Show, don't tell" is the oldest conversion principle. Medium-high impact, medium effort.
