## Trust Builders (What's Working)

### 1. **Transparent Pricing with a $0 Entry Point**
The three-tier structure — **Signals at $0/mo**, **Signals + Actions at $499/mo**, and **Signals + Actions + Member App at $1,499/mo** — is clearly laid out. This is rare in B2B SaaS for private clubs and immediately distinguishes Swoop from competitors who hide behind "Request a Demo." The freemium tier labeled "Start for zero" directly reduces perceived risk.

### 2. **Named Testimonial with Real Attribution**
The quote from **"Jarvis McDonald, GM, Pinetree Country Club, 500+ member boarding, private club"** is one of the strongest trust elements on the page. A real name, real title, real club name, and club size — this is verifiable. A buyer can look up Pinetree Country Club and Jarvis McDonald on LinkedIn. The quote itself references specific outcomes: "we connected them a dinner, happy hour, and earned $215 in annual dues. The Board was thrilled."

### 3. **Interactive ROI Calculator**
The "Find out exactly how much dues revenue is walking out your door" section with adjustable sliders for **Dues Protected (15)**, showing **$120,000** in at-risk revenue and **$80,000** in recoverable revenue with Swoop, is a strong transparency tool. It allows the buyer to input their own club's numbers rather than accepting Swoop's projections at face value. The breakdown showing "Signals + Actions your cost: $6,108" vs. "Net savings: $74,812" with the note "This shows your commitment" gives the buyer agency.

### 4. **Specific Headline Stats**
- **"65%"** — "Avg. at-risk member retention rate. Based on 2024 cohort data"
- **"$74K"** — "Avg. mean savings per club in first 90 days. Source: Swoop $215 cohort"

These are more specific than typical SaaS marketing. The timeframes ("first 90 days," "2024 cohort") and the denominators ("5 of 7") add credibility. The "5 of 7" is especially interesting because it's not 100% — admitting that 2 of 7 didn't hit the mark in 60 days is a subtle honesty signal.

### 5. **"Learn in 14 days. Zero if Rejected. Cancel any Time."**
This line directly beneath the pricing tiers addresses three core buyer objections: commitment length, what happens if it doesn't work, and lock-in. This is a meaningful risk-reduction statement.

### 6. **FAQ Section Labeled "Things GMs Ask Us"**

## Trust Killers (What's Hurting)

### 1. **Headline Claim Without Linked Proof — CRITICAL**
"5 of 7 founding partner clubs recovered Swoop's annual cost within 60 days of their first intervention (2024 cohort)" is the central value proposition of the page. Yet:
- **No clubs are named** (except Pinetree in the testimonial below, which is not explicitly linked to this stat)
- **No case study links** are provided
- **No methodology** for how "recovered cost" was measured
- **"Founding partner clubs"** is an undefined term — is this a special cohort? Were they hand-held? Would a normal customer see the same results?

A skeptical GM will immediately ask: "Which clubs? Can I call them?" The absence of this proof is the page's single biggest credibility gap.

### 2. **Stat Source Citations Are Vague**
- "65% Avg. at-risk member retention rate" cites "Based on 2024 cohort data" — What cohort? How many clubs? How many members? What's the baseline retention rate without Swoop?
- "$74K Avg. mean savings per club in first 90 days" cites "Source: Swoop $215 cohort" — What is the "$215 cohort"? This label is cryptic and unexplained. Is this referring to the $215 in the Jarvis testimonial? That would be a single data point masquerading as a cohort metric.
- These sources sound specific but are actually self-referential and unverifiable.

### 3. **ROI Calculator Assumptions Are Hidden**
The calculator shows dramatic numbers ($120,000 at-risk, $80,000 recoverable) but the underlying assumptions aren't transparent on the visible page:
- What is the assumed average dues per member?
- What is the assumed churn reduction rate?
- How is "at-risk" defined?
- The small text below the calculator ("Avg this based on a 4% churn rate...with a cost of member acquisition...") is partially visible but not fully legible in the screenshot, suggesting the fine print exists but may be too small or truncated to be read easily.

### 4. **Only One Testimonial on the Entire Pricing Page**
For a page asking for up to $1,499/mo ($17,988/year), a single testimonial — even a well-attributed one — is insufficient. Where are:
- Multiple GM voices from different club types (golf, country, city, yacht)?
- Board-level endorsements (since the testimonial itself mentions "The Board was thrilled")?
- Quantified outcomes from multiple clubs?

### 5. **No Product Screenshots or Demo Artifacts**
The pricing page describes features ("AI-powered health scores," "churn prediction alerts," "personalized action plans") but shows no actual product UI. The ROI calculator chart is a marketing visualization, not a product screenshot. A buyer at the $1,499/mo tier wants to see the member app, the dashboard, the alert system. The only CTA is "Book a 30-Min Walkthrough" — which means the product is a complete black box until the sales call.

### 6. **"Zero Implementation Fees" Claim Needs Substantiation**
The line "Zero implementation fees. Swoop's onboarding team maps your mesh only into zero gate, zero legacy integrations, zero setup cost. No hidden fees for onboarding." — this is a strong claim. But:
- What does implementation actually involve?
- How long does onboarding take?
- What if my club uses a PMS that isn't supported?
- The claim "25 integrations, Jonas, Northstar, Club Essentials, SG, resort reservations, 5 meals, CSV imports" partially addresses this, but "CSV imports" as a catch-all could mean manual, error-prone data entry.

### 7. **"$499/mo" Middle Tier Lacks Social Proof Anchor**
There's no indicator of which tier is most popular ("Most Popular" badge is absent), no customer count per tier, and no indication of how many clubs are currently using Swoop at all. A buyer doesn't know if they're joining a platform with 7 clubs or 700.

### 8. **The Vercel Subdomain URL**
The site is hosted at `swoop-member-intelligence-website.vercel.app` — a Vercel deployment URL rather than a custom domain. This is a significant legitimacy red flag for a sophisticated buyer. It suggests the site may be a staging environment, a prototype, or a company that hasn't invested in basic web infrastructure. This alone would make some buyers pause.

## The Due Diligence Gaps

A careful buyer would leave this page and immediately research the following:

1. **"Who are the 7 founding partner clubs?"** — The buyer would search for Swoop's customer list, look for press releases about partnerships, and try to identify the clubs beyond Pinetree.

2. **"Does Jarvis McDonald actually exist and work at Pinetree Country Club?"** — LinkedIn search for Jarvis McDonald, Pinetree Country Club verification. This is the single most verifiable claim on the page.

3. **"Who founded Swoop and what's their background?"** — No founder names or team information visible on this pricing page. The buyer would click "About" in the nav or search LinkedIn for "Swoop Club Intelligence" to see the team.

4. **"What do other GMs say about Swoop outside of Swoop's own site?"** — The buyer would search for Swoop reviews on Club Benchmarking forums, CMAA community boards, Golf Business magazine, or any independent review site.

5. **"How does the $74K savings figure actually work?"** — The buyer would want to reverse-engineer the math: If a club has 500 members at $10,000/year dues, and Swoop retains 15 members who would have left, that's $150K — so $74K "savings" implies a retention rate or attribution methodology that isn't explained.

6. **"What happens to my data if I cancel?"** — The FAQ mentions cancellation but the answer isn't fully visible. Data portability and deletion policies are critical for club GMs who handle sensitive member information.

7. **"Does Swoop integrate with MY specific club management system?"** — The mention of Jonas, Northstar, Club Essentials, and SG is good, but a buyer running Clubessential (different from Club Essentials?), Ten-Four, or MembersFirst would need to verify compatibility.

8. **"Is this a real company or a startup that might not exist in 12 months?"** — The Vercel URL, the small founding cohort (7 clubs), and the absence of funding announcements, press coverage, or employee count would push a risk-averse GM to investigate longevity and financial stability.

9. **"What does 'Zero if Rejected' actually mean legally?"** — Is there a money-back guarantee? What constitutes "rejected"? Who decides? This needs contractual clarity that isn't provided on the page.

10. **"Can I talk to a reference club before committing?"** — The page provides no mechanism for reference calls, which is standard in B2B sales at this price point. The buyer would expect to request — and receive — 2-3 reference contacts during the sales process.
