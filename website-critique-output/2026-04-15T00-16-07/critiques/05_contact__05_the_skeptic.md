## The Due Diligence Gaps

A careful buyer would leave this page and immediately research:

1. **"Swoop Golf Inc" — Does this company actually exist?** State incorporation records, LinkedIn company page, Crunchbase, etc. The site gives almost nothing to validate corporate existence.

2. **"Robert Torres Meridian Hills CC"** — Is this a real person? Is he still the GM there? Does the club actually use Swoop? This is the one verifiable claim, so a skeptic will verify it.

3. **"SOC 2 Type II (Audit Active)"** — Who is the auditor? When was the last report? Can I request a copy? The page claims it but provides no way to verify. A real SOC 2 report should be available on request.

4. **Who are the founders/leadership?** No names appear anywhere. A buyer will search LinkedIn for "Swoop Golf" employees to see who's behind this.

5. **How long has this company been operating?** No founding date, no history, no milestones. Is this a 5-year-old company or a 5-week-old company?

6. **What do the other 6 founding-partner clubs think?** Only Meridian Hills CC is referenced via testimonial. Are any others willing to serve as references?

7. **What does the actual product UI look like?** A buyer who's been burned before will want to see the dashboard before a call, or at minimum a 2-minute video.

8. **Is swoopgolf.com a real domain?** The email references it, but the page is hosted on Vercel. A buyer would check if the primary domain resolves and looks legitimate.

9. **CMAA, NCA, or other industry affiliations?** Club GMs trust vendors who are embedded in their industry ecosystem. No association logos, no conference mentions, no industry publication features are cited.

10. **What happens if I don't like what I see on the 30-minute call?** The "deleted within 30 days" language is good, but a cautious buyer would want this in writing (the mutual NDA mention helps, but is it a real NDA template they can review?).
