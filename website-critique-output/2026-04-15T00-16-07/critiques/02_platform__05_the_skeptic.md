## The Due Diligence Gaps

A careful club GM would leave this site and immediately:

1. **Search "Swoop Club Intelligence reviews"** — looking for any third-party validation that this product exists and works. They will likely find nothing, which is worse than finding mixed reviews.

2. **Search "Swoop Club Intelligence founder" / LinkedIn** — trying to determine who is behind this, what their club industry experience is, and whether they're credible operators or tech tourists.

3. **Ask their peer network:** "Has anyone used Swoop?" — and the absence of any named reference club on the site means the answer will almost certainly be "Never heard of it."

4. **Look for the actual domain** — the Vercel staging URL will make them question whether `swoopclub.com` or similar exists, and if not, why not.

5. **Ask about data security** — clubs handle sensitive member financial and personal data. There's no mention of SOC 2, data encryption, GDPR/CCPA compliance, data residency, or security practices anywhere visible.

6. **Ask about integration specifics** — "Which POS systems? Which tee sheet providers? Jonas? Northstar? Club Essentials? ForeTees?" The page is category-level, not vendor-specific.

7. **Ask about implementation** — "Typical launch: IT involved: Zero" is a bold claim. How? What does onboarding look like? How long until value is realized? Who is my point of contact?

8. **Ask about contract terms** — Annual? Monthly? What happens to my data if I cancel? What's the commitment?

9. **Look for the company's legal entity** — Is this a Delaware LLC? When was it incorporated? Any funding? Any Crunchbase presence?

10. **Ask what "AI" actually means here** — Is this GPT-4 with club data piped in? Custom models? Rule-based logic branded as AI? The page makes AI the central value proposition but provides zero technical transparency.
