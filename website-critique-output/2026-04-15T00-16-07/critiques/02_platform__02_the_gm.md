## What Would Make Me Move Forward

1. **Show me one real club by name.** Just one. "At [Specific Private Club], Swoop identified 14 at-risk members in the first 30 days — the GM retained 11 of them, protecting $67K in annual dues." That single sentence would move this from "interesting concept" to "this actually works." Right now the "$12k / 14 / $47k" numbers floating without attribution feel like marketing math.

2. **Give me a pricing ballpark.** I'm not asking for a detailed quote — I'm asking: "Is this $500/month or $5,000/month?" I need to know if this is even in my budget range before I give you my email. A simple "Plans start at $X/month for clubs with 300–500 members" would save both of us time.

3. **Name my POS and tee sheet explicitly.** The integration diagram near the bottom seems to show system categories, but I want to see "Works with Jonas, Northstar, Club Prophet, ForeTees, Chelsea" in plain text. If you integrate with my stack, say so. If you don't, I'd rather know now.

4. **Add a "What your members see (and don't see)" section.** My biggest fear is that this becomes another app I have to explain to a 72-year-old member who still prints his email. If Swoop is invisible to members — if it's a staff-side intelligence layer — say that explicitly and prominently. That's actually a huge selling point.

5. **Give me a timeline.** "Most clubs are live within 2–3 weeks" or whatever it is. The "Typical launch" section at the bottom hints at a process but gives me no timeframe. I plan purchases around board meeting cycles — I need to know if this is a Q3 or Q4 initiative.

## What Would Make Me Leave

1. **The complete absence of social proof.** I've been burned before. Two vendors showed me beautiful demos, promised the world, and delivered headaches. Every claim on this page is self-asserted. "Six AI agents working your club — live" — live WHERE? At which clubs? The "daily brief, written overnight" — who's actually receiving these? Without a single named reference, my skepticism wins and I close the tab before filling out any form.

2. **The AI language, while restrained, still makes me nervous.** "Six AI agents working your club" — my board chair is going to ask "what does that actually mean?" and "who's liable if the AI flags a member incorrectly and we act on bad information?" The page doesn't address AI accuracy, human oversight, or what happens when the system gets something wrong. For a product that's essentially telling me to make operational decisions based on its recommendations, that's a gap I can't ignore.

3. **No indication of what happens after I click the CTA.** "See it. No pitch required." — see what, exactly? A recorded demo? A live walkthrough? A sandbox? Am I going to fill out a form and then get 47 emails from a BDR named Tyler? The ambiguity, even though the tone is friendly, still triggers my vendor-fatigue alarm.

## The Board Test

Then he'd scroll, appreciate the clean design and plain language, nod at the daily brief concept. But about 60 seconds in, he'd text me back: **"Who else uses this?"**

And I'd have nothing to tell him. No club names. No case study. No testimonial. No logo bar. Nothing.

The site's design is professional enough that it wouldn't embarrass me — this isn't some janky startup page with stock photos of people high-fiving. The visual identity is clean, the orange/cream palette feels premium without being stuffy, and the copy is genuinely well-written for our world. But my board doesn't approve vendors based on nice copy. They approve vendors based on peer validation and quantifiable ROI.

**Right now, this site would make my board say: "Looks promising. Who do we know that's using it? Find out and report back."** And if I can't answer that, the conversation dies.

The product concept is strong. The positioning is excellent. The language feels like it was written by someone who understands private club operations, not a SaaS copywriter who Googled "country club pain points." But trust is built with evidence, and this page is asking me to trust the concept without showing me the proof. Give me one named club, one real result, and one honest pricing range — and this moves from my "interesting" bookmark folder to my next board agenda.
