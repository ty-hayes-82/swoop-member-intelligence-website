**What's working:**

**#1 Fix:** Add a specificity layer: one line of social proof directly beneath the CTA (*"Used by 40+ private clubs | Average 12% churn reduction in 90 days"*) and ensure the headline or subhead explicitly names the ICP ("for private club GMs").

### Summary

Swoop's Platform page is a **well-crafted product narrative** with genuinely sharp copywriting (especially "Take a dollar number to the board" and "Six jobs before your morning coffee"). The strategic positioning is strong — the integration-not-replacement angle, the security section, the daily brief concept. This is written by someone who deeply understands the buyer.

But it's architecturally broken as a conversion engine. Two CTAs on a page this long, zero named social proof, no micro-conversion alternatives, and no pricing signals means you're relying entirely on the small percentage of visitors who (a) read the entire page, (b) are ready to talk to sales right now, and (c) trust an unverified product enough to book a call. Fix the mid-page CTA desert and add one named testimonial, and you likely see a 30-50% improvement in demo requests within 30 days.
