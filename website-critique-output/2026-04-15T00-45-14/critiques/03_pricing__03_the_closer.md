### POST-CALCULATOR CTA ZONE

**Page Job:** Convert the conviction built by the calculator into a booked walkthrough.

**Is it doing the job?** No — this is the biggest conversion leak on the page.

**What's broken:**
- There are TWO equally-weighted CTAs stacked vertically: **"Book a Walkthrough With Your Members"** (orange filled button) and **"Print this ROI report for my Board"** (orange outline button). These serve fundamentally different buyer readiness levels but are presented as equals, creating Hick's Law decision paralysis.
- The copy above — *"Plug in a club like yours at $1,199/year. Your club's risk alone could be 5-10X that."* — is doing important framing work but feels like an afterthought. It should be a larger, styled callout.
- The testimonial quote from "Landon Wilkerson, VP, Pantera Country Club, WA — member founding partner club" is strong and specific, but it's sandwiched between the calculator and the CTAs with minimal visual distinction. It should be a clearly designed testimonial card with a photo, club logo, and the specific metric it references.

**#1 Fix:** Establish clear CTA hierarchy. Make "Book a Walkthrough With Your Members" the dominant orange button at full width. Move "Print this ROI report for my Board" to a smaller text link or secondary gray button below it, framed as: *"Not ready to book? Print this ROI report to share with your Board →"*. This gives the researcher an escape valve without competing with the primary conversion action.

### PRICING TIERS SECTION

**Page Job:** Help the visitor self-select a tier and move toward purchase.

**Is it doing the job?** Partially — the tier structure is smart, but execution creates unnecessary friction.

**What's working:**
- **"Start at zero. Upgrade when the math shows up."** — Outstanding section headline. It removes 100% of financial risk objection and leverages the commitment/consistency principle (start small, upgrade when convinced).
- *"No strings. Never costs cash. Cancel at the end of any month."* — Direct objection handling. Good.
- The $0/mo Signals tier as a freemium entry point is excellent product-led growth strategy.
- The "FOUNDING MEMBER PRICING" label on the $499/mo tier creates authentic scarcity and exclusivity.
- The $499/mo tier is visually highlighted with the orange "Founding member pricing" badge, correctly anchoring it as the recommended option.
- **"Zero Implementation Fees"** callout below pricing — strong objection handler.

**What's broken:**
- The three tiers ($0/mo, $499/mo, $1,499/mo) have dense feature lists in small text that are hard to scan from the screenshot. B2B buyers at the pricing stage need to quickly understand the delta between tiers, not read feature laundry lists.
- The CTAs within tiers are inconsistent: "Get Free Daily Alerts" for $0, "Book a 30-Min Walkthrough" for $499, "See the Full Platform" for $1,499. Three different actions with three different commitment levels. The $1,499 tier says "See the Full Platform" — which is lower commitment than the $499 tier's "Book a 30-Min Walkthrough." This is backwards. Your highest tier should have the highest-conviction CTA.
- There is **no social proof at the tier level**. Not a single "X clubs on this plan" or "Most popular" badge based on actual data. At the moment of pricing evaluation — the highest-anxiety decision point — you've removed all social proof.
- The line *"Zero Implementation Fees. Swoop's onboarding team maps your existing tech in 48 hrs, zero setup to you or your team. No hidden API invoices."* is buried below the tiers. This is a top-3 objection handler (implementation pain) and should be prominently placed *within* or *above* the tier cards.

**#1 Fix:** Add a proof point directly into the $499/mo tier card — something like "Chosen by 5 of 7 founding clubs" or "Avg. club recovers cost in <60 days." Place it immediately below the price. This applies authority and social proof at the exact decision point (Cialdini's social proof principle). The absence of any proof element in the pricing cards is the second-biggest conversion leak on the page.

**Page Job:** Handle residual objections that prevent conversion.

**Is it doing the job?** Partially — the framing is good, but the coverage is thin.

**What's working:**
- The title "Things GMs ask us." is persona-specific and conversational. It signals "this is for you" to the target buyer.
- "Do I need to replace my current software?" — Excellent question. The answer mentions 18 integrations and names specific systems. This is a real buyer question, not a softball.
- "Is my members' data secure?" — Another real objection addressed directly.

**What's broken:**

These are critical conversion questions — a GM considering a "founding partner" tier needs to know what that means, and the cancellation question is the #1 risk objection for any subscription.

**#1 Fix:** Expand the FAQ to 6-8 questions, ensure all are expanded by default (or at least the top 4), and add a question specifically addressing ROI skepticism: *"What if we don't recover the cost?"* with an answer that references the 5-of-7 stat and the $0 starting tier as a risk-free entry point. This closes the loop on the page's entire narrative.
