## The Rewrite List

### 1. Hero Headline — First Line Lacks Teeth

**Current:** `"Get a Custom Retention Plan."`

**Why it fails:** "Custom Retention Plan" is accurate but reads like a deliverable description, not a provocation. It doesn't name the pain. A GM scanning this doesn't feel *seen* — they feel categorized. Compare it to the second line, "Not a Pitch Deck," which is visceral and oppositional. The first line needs to earn that contrast.

**Suggested rewrite:**
> **"See Exactly Where Your Club Is Bleeding Revenue.**
> **Not a Pitch Deck."**

Alternative:
> **"Find the Members You're About to Lose.**
> **Not a Pitch Deck."**

---

### 2. Subheadline — Slightly Over-Packed

**Current:** `"In 30 minutes, we load your tee-sheet data into Swoop and show you exactly where revenue is leaking and which members are quietly disengaging. You leave with a prioritized action list — not a pitch deck."`

**Why it fails:** This is strong but it repeats "not a pitch deck" from the headline, which dilutes the punch of that line. Also, "load your tee-sheet data into Swoop" is process-oriented — it puts the product name before the outcome. And the sentence is 42 words, which is long for a subheadline.

**Suggested rewrite:**
> "We connect to your tee sheet and POS, and in 30 minutes show you which members are quietly disengaging and where revenue is leaking. You leave with a ranked action list — yours to keep, whether you buy or not."

### 3. CTA Button — Good But Could Be More Specific

**Current:** `"Get My Custom Retention Plan"`

**Why it fails:** It doesn't fail — it's significantly better than "Book a Demo" or "Get Started." But "Custom Retention Plan" is abstract enough that a busy GM might not feel the urgency. The page already establishes a 30-minute, data-driven walkthrough — the CTA should echo that specificity.

**Suggested rewrite:**
> **"Show Me My Club's Blind Spots"**

Or, to maintain the deliverable framing:
> **"Get My 90-Day Action Plan — Free"**

(This borrows from the checklist item about the 90-day action plan, which is the most tangible deliverable mentioned and isn't currently reflected in the CTA.)

### 4. Bottom Section Headline — Strong But Could Be Sharper

The real power of this section is its specificity — tee sheet leakage, at-risk members, F&B staffing pressure. The headline should set up that specificity rather than abstract it.

### 5. Process Transparency — Step 2 Buries Key Trust Signal

**Why it fails:** "Zero IT required" is a *massive* objection-killer for GMs who've been burned by tech implementations that require board approval and IT involvement. **Suggested rewrite — elevate the phrase:**

Or integrate into the hero subheadline:

## The Missing Story

**What's absent: The "Before Swoop" Nightmare Scenario.**

This page does a masterful job of telling you what you'll *get* (ranked gaps, benchmarks, 90-day plan) and offering proof (the Torres quote). But it never paints the picture of *what life looks like without this call* — the GM who finds out about member attrition from a resignation letter instead of a dashboard. The board meeting where you can't explain why F&B revenue is down 12%. The spreadsheet you built at 11pm to manually track tee sheet utilization.

A 2–3 sentence "before" vignette at the top of the page — or even a single line like *"Most GMs find out a member is leaving after the resignation letter. Swoop flags them 90 days before."* — would create the emotional contrast that makes the rest of the page land harder.

The Robert Torres quote partially fills this gap ("Swoop flagged a member we were about to lose"), but it's positioned as proof, not as the emotional hook. Moving the *concept* of that story earlier — before the form, before the deliverables — would transform this from a very good demo page into one that makes a GM feel like not booking the call is the risky decision.

Additionally, one quote from one GM at one club is doing a lot of heavy lifting. A second voice — different club size, different pain point (F&B vs. retention vs. tee sheet utilization) — would reinforce that this isn't a one-club success story. Even a one-liner: *"We saved 40 hours a month on manual reporting." — Director of Operations, 600-member equity club* would add dimensionality.
