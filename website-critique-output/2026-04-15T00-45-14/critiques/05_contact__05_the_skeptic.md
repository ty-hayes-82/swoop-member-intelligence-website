## The Due Diligence Gaps

A skeptical GM or COO would immediately go research the following after visiting this page:

1. **"Swoop Golf" on LinkedIn** — Does the company page exist? How many employees? Who are the founders? What are their backgrounds? Is this a 2-person startup or a funded company?

2. **"Robert Torres Meridian Hills CC"** — Is this a real person? Is he actually the GM there? Would he confirm the $18K claim? This is the single most verifiable assertion on the page.

3. **"Swoop Golf SOC 2"** — Is there any third-party evidence of this audit? A trust portal? An auditor's name? If this can't be externally verified, it significantly damages the SOC 2 claim's value.

4. **"Swoop Golf reviews" / "Swoop Golf private clubs"** — Any third-party coverage? Club management forum discussions? CMAA mentions? Conference presentations?

5. **What does the product actually look like?** — The buyer would search for product screenshots, demo videos on YouTube, or G2/Capterra listings.

6. **How much does it cost?** — The buyer may email demo@swoopgolf.com asking for ballpark pricing before committing to a call.

7. **Who are the other 6 founding-partner clubs?** — Meridian Hills may be one, but who are the rest? A buyer wants to call a peer.

8. **Is "swoopgolf.com" a real domain with history?** — A WHOIS lookup would reveal registration date, which tells you if this company has existed for years or weeks.

9. **Does Swoop have actual integrations with Jonas/Clubessential/Northstar, or are these aspirational?** — A technical buyer or IT director would want documentation or confirmation from the PMS vendor.
