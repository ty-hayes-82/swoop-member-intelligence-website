## Narrative Verdict

This pricing page is doing something genuinely rare and smart: it leads with a dollar-amount headline tied to the buyer's pain, offers an interactive ROI calculator before ever showing a price, and then presents a "start at zero" freemium model that de-risks the entire purchase decision. The narrative architecture is strong — problem → quantified pain → interactive proof → pricing → FAQ. However, the page undermines its own excellent strategic choices with inconsistent copy precision in the mid-to-lower sections, generic CTA language in places, and a few missed opportunities to let member voices or real club stories carry the emotional weight. **The single most impactful change:** replace every instance of "Book a Walkthrough" with a CTA that names the outcome ("See Your At-Risk Members in 10 Minutes") — the current label sounds like a vendor's ask, not a buyer's reward.

## The Rewrite List

### 1. Hero Subheadline
**Current:** *"5 of 7 founding partner clubs recovered Swoop's annual cost within 60 days of their first intervention (2024 cohort). Start free. Upgrade when the ROI shows up in your own numbers."*

**Why it fails:** This is trying to do too much in one breath. It packs a stat, a proof window, a cohort qualifier, and two separate CTAs into a single run-on thought. The parenthetical "(2024 cohort)" reads like a footnote in a sentence that should feel like a confident handshake. A busy GM scanning this will absorb maybe 40% of it.

**Suggested rewrite:**
*"5 of 7 partner clubs recovered Swoop's full annual cost in under 60 days. Start free — upgrade when your numbers prove it."*

---

### 2. Primary CTA Button (Hero)
**Current:** *"Calculate your ROI"*

**Why it fails:** It's decent — it's action-oriented and specific. But it frames the action as work the GM has to do ("you calculate"), rather than a reward they receive. Minor, but this is prime real estate.

**Suggested rewrite:**
*"See What Your Club Is Losing"*

---

### 3. Pricing Tier: Signals + Actions ($499/mo) Description
**Current:** *"Everything in Signals, plus Swoop writes the outreach, campaigns, and reports for you using Oyster — so your team doesn't have to start from scratch."*

**Why it fails:** "Oyster" is introduced without context — is it an AI? A feature? A tool name? The phrase "doesn't have to start from scratch" is vague and undermines the specificity that the rest of the page earns. "Writes the outreach, campaigns, and reports" is a feature list masquerading as a benefit.

**Suggested rewrite:**
*"Swoop's AI writes your outreach emails, re-engagement campaigns, and board reports automatically — so your team acts on intelligence instead of building spreadsheets."*

---

### 4. Repeated CTA: "Book a Walkthrough" / "Book a 30-Min Walkthrough"
**Current:** Appears at least 3 times on the page — in the pricing section, in the lower pricing card, and in the final CTA block.

**Why it fails:** "Walkthrough" is vendor language. It tells the GM what *you* want to do (walk them through your product). It doesn't tell them what *they* get. And "30-min" signals a time cost, not a value gain. By the third repetition, it feels like you're asking for the same meeting they've been avoiding from every other SaaS vendor.

**Suggested rewrite (vary by placement):**
- Pricing section: *"See Your Club's Risk Score — Free"*
- Lower card: *"Watch How Pinetree Recovered $74K"*
- Final CTA: *"See Which Members Are at Risk"*

---

### 5. Final CTA Section Headline
**Current:** *"Ready to see which of your members are at risk?"*

**Why it fails:** It's not bad — it's specific and outcome-oriented. But it's phrased as a yes/no question, which psychologically invites "not yet" as a valid answer. The subtext beneath it — *"No signup. No demo-to-close. Your first conversation with Swoop won't feel like someone's trying to sell you."* — is doing the real persuasive work but is buried in small text.

**Suggested rewrite:**
Headline: *"Your at-risk members aren't waiting. Neither should you."*
Subheadline: *"No signup required. No demo-to-close pressure. Just your club's data telling a story you need to hear."*

## The Missing Story

**What's absent: A day-in-the-life before/after scenario.**

The page does an excellent job with *numbers* — $74K, 65%, 5 of 7, the interactive calculator. It has one testimonial quote (attributed to what appears to be a GM at Pinetree Country Club mentioning the "founding partner" experience). But there is no *narrative* that lets a GM see themselves in the product.

Imagine a short section between the calculator and the pricing tiers:

> **Before Swoop:** *"Every quarter, your board asks why membership is down. You pull reports from three systems, guess at who's disengaged, and hope your gut is right. By the time you spot a resignation letter, the member's been gone for months — you just didn't know it."*
>
> **After Swoop:** *"Tuesday morning, you open one dashboard. Fourteen members flagged as at-risk — with the specific signals that triggered each flag. By lunch, personalized outreach is already sent. By quarter's end, your board sees a retention chart that trends up for the first time in three years."*

This kind of scenario would bridge the gap between the impressive math and the emotional reality of a GM's daily life. The page currently convinces the *analytical* brain but doesn't fully capture the *experiential* brain — the GM who's tired of being blindsided at board meetings, who's embarrassed when a 15-year member leaves and nobody saw it coming.

The testimonial from the founding partner club is a start, but it's doing solo duty. A second voice — ideally a quote about the *feeling* of having intelligence rather than the *metric* of having it — would complete the emotional circuit. Something like: *"For the first time, I walked into a board meeting knowing exactly which members needed attention and what we'd already done about it."*

That's the missing story: not what Swoop does, but what it *feels like* to have Swoop working for you.
