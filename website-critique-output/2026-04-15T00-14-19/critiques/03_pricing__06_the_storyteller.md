## The Rewrite List

### 1. Pricing Tier Name: "Signals"

**Current text:** "Signals — $0/mo — Ready-only alerts. Understand your systems and a human-first step, no install, and start for no risk at all."

**Why it fails:** "Ready-only alerts" (likely "Read-only") is a technical descriptor that means nothing to a GM. "Understand your systems" is vague. "Human-first step" is unclear jargon. The sentence is also syntactically broken.

**Suggested rewrite:** "Signals — $0/mo — See which members are drifting before they resign. Daily alerts straight to your inbox — no software to install, no commitment, no risk."

## The Missing Story

**What's absent: A single, specific before/after club narrative.**

The page has great structural elements — the calculator, the "5 of 7" stat, the Laura McDonald quote. But it's missing the one story that would make a GM feel this in their gut: **a day-in-the-life or before/after vignette of a real club.**

Something like:

> *"Pinetree Country Club had 12 members resign in Q1 2023. Their GM, Laura McDonald, had no warning on any of them. After deploying Swoop's Signals tier — free — she started getting daily alerts. In the first 90 days, her team personally reached out to 9 flagged members. 7 stayed. One upgraded to a family membership. Pinetree recovered Swoop's annual cost in 47 days and moved to the full platform."*

This isn't a testimonial. It's a **narrative case proof** — the most powerful persuasion tool for a B2B buyer who's about to put their reputation on the line with their board. The page builds a financial argument beautifully, but it never shows a single GM making the decision and winning. That's the story that turns "this looks interesting" into "I need to book this call today."

A secondary missing element: **the board-ready moment.** GMs don't just need to be convinced — they need ammunition to convince their board or finance committee. A line like "Every Swoop customer gets a board-ready ROI summary in their first 60 days" would directly address the internal sell that every GM has to navigate. The FAQ hints at this ("What does the founding partner program actually look like?") but never delivers the reassurance explicitly.
