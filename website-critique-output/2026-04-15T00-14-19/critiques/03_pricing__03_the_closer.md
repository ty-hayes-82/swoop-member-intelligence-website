# Conversion Audit: Swoop Club Intelligence — Pricing Page

## Conversion Verdict

This page is doing something genuinely rare and smart: it leads with an ROI calculator and frames pricing as a cost-recovery conversation rather than a cost conversation. That's elite positioning. However, the page is trying to do the jobs of **four pages at once** (hero/value prop, ROI calculator, pricing tiers, FAQ) which dilutes the conversion gravity at each decision point and creates scroll fatigue before a visitor reaches the actual pricing. **The single biggest conversion leak is that the ROI calculator — the most compelling conversion tool on the page — sits above the pricing tiers, meaning visitors who came specifically to evaluate pricing must scroll past a lengthy interactive section before they can compare plans, and many will bounce before getting there.**

## The Conversion Audit

### HERO SECTION (Top of Page)

**Page Job:** Immediately frame Swoop's pricing as an investment with guaranteed ROI, not a cost.

**Is it doing that job?** Mostly yes. "Recover your software costs in 60 days. Start for zero." is a strong, outcome-oriented headline. The "PRICING" label above the headline correctly orients the visitor. The subhead — "5 of 7 founding-partner clubs recovered Swoop's annual cost within 60 days of their first intervention (2024 cohort). Start free. Upgrade when the ROI shows up in your own numbers." — is specific, time-bound proof that builds immediate credibility.

**What's working:**
- The three orange stat cards ("65%", "$74K", "5 of 7") provide immediate quantitative proof using the authority principle.
- "Start for zero" eliminates the price-anchoring fear before they've even scrolled.
- The orange "Calculate your ROI" CTA is visually dominant and action-oriented.

**#1 Fix:** The stat cards' supporting context text (the labels beneath 65%, $74K, 5 of 7) is too small to read in the screenshot and likely too small at normal viewport sizes. These are your most persuasive proof points and the context is critical. **Increase the supporting label font size to at least 14px and make the descriptions explicit** — e.g., instead of tiny grey text, make it "65% — Average at-risk member retention rate" in legible, high-contrast type. Without readable context, these numbers are just numbers — the Cialdini authority/specificity principle only works if the visitor can process the claim.

### ROI CALCULATOR SECTION

**Page Job:** Convert abstract pricing into a personalized, concrete dollar-value conversation unique to each visitor's club.

**Is it doing that job?** This is the **strongest conversion element on the entire page** and a genuine competitive moat. The interactive calculator with "DUES PROTECTED" on the left and the personalized output ("$120,000" at-risk dues, "$80,000" Swoop recovers) on the right is brilliant. The slider inputs (Dues Protected: 15, showing "$120,000 At-risk dues per year") make the value tangible. The output panel showing "Signals + Actions your cost: $6,100 / Net savings: $74,812" directly addresses the ROI objection before it's even raised.

The testimonial quote from "Jared McIlwain, GM, Preston Country Club - 500+ member founding partner club" placed right below the calculator is perfectly positioned (social proof at the moment of evaluation — textbook placement).

**What's working:**
- Personalization via sliders creates the IKEA effect — visitors who build their own ROI estimate are more committed to the outcome (commitment/consistency principle).
- The "WITH SWOOP" panel showing the dollar recovery vs. cost creates an irresistible value frame.
- The "Ready to recover your at-risk dues?" micro-headline below the calculator is a well-timed nudge.
- Two CTAs with different commitment levels: "Book a Walkthrough With Your Numbers" (orange, primary) and "Find this ROI report to my Board" (outline, secondary) — this is smart segmentation for the GM who needs board approval vs. the GM who has buying authority.

**#1 Fix:** Move a **condensed version** of the pricing tiers ABOVE the calculator, or add anchor links ("See Plans" / "Calculate Your ROI") at the top of the page so pricing-ready visitors can jump directly to tier comparison. Currently, a GM who came to this page from a "Pricing" nav click has to scroll through the entire calculator section to see actual plan costs. This violates the principle of matching page content to visitor intent — the nav link promised "Pricing" and the page delivers "ROI justification" first. Many pricing-intent visitors will bounce. **Alternatively, add a sticky anchor bar below the hero with two tabs: "ROI Calculator" and "Plans & Pricing."**

### PRICING TIERS SECTION

**Page Job:** Enable plan comparison, anchor on value, and drive visitors to the right-fit plan with a clear upgrade path.

**Is it doing that job?** Partially. The section headline "Start at zero. Upgrade when the math shows up." is excellent — it reframes the free tier as a no-risk starting point and the paid tiers as an evidence-based upgrade, which defuses the "what if it doesn't work" objection elegantly.

The three tiers are clearly structured:
- **Signals: $0/mo** — "Ready-only alerts. Understand your at-risk members and their at-risk revenue…"
- **Signals + Actions: $499/mo** — "Everything in Signals, plus the outreach, scripts for, and staffing plans to act on what's flagged…"
- **Signals + Actions + Member App: $1,499/mo** — "Add a fully-branded member app..."

The "Learn in 14 days. Zero if Negative. Cancel any time." banner above the tiers is a powerful risk-reversal statement addressing three objections simultaneously (time to value, ROI guarantee, lock-in fear).

**What's working:**
- The $0 entry point makes the commitment ladder easy to start (Fogg model: high motivation + low ability barrier = action).
- Feature lists with checkmarks provide scannable comparison.
- The middle tier ($499) appears to be the recommended plan, which is a good price-anchoring strategy.
- "Cancel at the end of any month" removes lock-in objection.
- "No long-term contract" stated clearly.

**What's NOT working:**
- There's no visual emphasis (border, badge, "Most Popular" tag) on the recommended tier. All three cards appear to have equal visual weight. The middle-tier highlighting convention exists because it works — it's anchoring + social proof ("most clubs choose this"). **Add a "Most Popular" or "Recommended" badge and a subtle border/shadow to the $499 tier.**
- The "Get Free Daily Alerts" CTA on the $0 tier is the weakest CTA copy on the page. "Start Free — See Your At-Risk Members" would be more outcome-oriented and create curiosity that drives activation.
- The "Book a 30-Min Walkthrough →" CTA on the middle and top tiers is fine but generic. Consider "Book a Walkthrough With Your Numbers →" (matching the calculator section's language) for consistency.
- The feature descriptions on each tier are dense and hard to scan in the screenshot. The font appears small, and the descriptions run into paragraphs rather than tight bullet points.

**#1 Fix:** **Add a "Most Popular" badge and visual differentiation to the $499 middle tier**, and change the $0 tier CTA from "Get Free Daily Alerts" to "Start Free — See Your At-Risk Members." These two changes alone will shift the distribution of tier selection toward the revenue-generating plans (anchoring principle + action-oriented CTA language).

### INTEGRATION/PLATFORM BAR

**Page Job:** Address the "does it work with our existing systems" objection.

**Is it doing that job?** There's a dark banner section mentioning "Zero implementation friction. Swoop's onboarding team maps your tech stack in zero..." with mentions of integrations (Northstar, Jonas/iClub, etc.) and "5 weeks, CSV imports." The "See the Full Platform" link is present.

**#1 Fix:** This section is easy to miss and visually compressed. The integration objection is one of the top 3 reasons club GMs hesitate on new software. **Break this out into a more prominent section with recognizable club management software logos (Jonas, Northstar, ClubProphet, etc.) displayed as a logo bar.** Logo bars leverage the authority principle — visual recognition is processed faster than text.

### FAQ SECTION ("Things GMs ask us")

**Page Job:** Handle remaining objections and build confidence for the conversion decision.

**Is it doing that job?** Partially. The questions visible are:
1. "Do I need to replace my current software?" — Answer: No, integrates with existing systems.
2. "Is my members' data secure?" — Answer addresses security.
3. "What does the founding partner program actually look like?"
4. "What happens if we cancel?"

The "GMs ask us" framing is good — it positions these as peer questions, not corporate FAQ fluff. The answers appear substantive for the first two visible ones.

**#1 Fix:** **Add 2-3 more questions that address the real buying-committee objections:** "How long until we see results?", "What does my staff need to do differently?", "Can I show my board a sample report before we start?" These are the questions a GM is rehearsing before they present to their board. The FAQ should arm them with answers. Also, ensure "What happens if we cancel?" has a visible, generous answer — right now it appears collapsed, and an unanswered cancellation question at the bottom of a pricing page is a trust leak.

### BOTTOM CTA SECTION

**Page Job:** Catch visitors who've scrolled the entire page and are now in a higher-conviction state.

**Is it doing that job?** Yes. "Ready to see which of your members are at risk?" is a well-crafted closing CTA headline that uses loss aversion (framing around risk to existing members, not gain from new software). The two CTAs — "Schedule a meeting" (text link) and "Book a 30-Min Walkthrough →" (orange button) — provide appropriate options.

**#1 Fix:** The body copy "Swoop takes 15 minutes. Your first member brief arrives tomorrow morning." is the most compelling urgency statement on the entire page — **this should be higher on the page, ideally in or near the hero section.** Time-to-value of "tomorrow morning" is a massive differentiator. It addresses the "how long until this works" objection and creates temporal urgency without manipulation. Consider moving this near the top or repeating it as a sticky banner element.

### Additional Notes

**What this page does exceptionally well:**
- The ROI calculator is a best-in-class conversion tool. It personalizes the value prop, creates investment in the outcome (commitment/consistency), and generates a shareable artifact ("Find this ROI report to my Board"). This is genuinely one of the better pricing page mechanics I've seen in vertical SaaS.
- The risk-reversal language ("Start for zero", "Learn in 14 days. Zero if Negative. Cancel any time.") is layered throughout the page, not isolated to one section. This compounds trust.
- The "Find this ROI report to my Board" secondary CTA shows deep understanding of the B2B buying process in this market — GMs rarely have unilateral purchasing authority. This CTA essentially creates a sales enablement document for your champion. Brilliant.

**What to watch for:**
- The sticky footer bar ("Tell us a Golf Pro is using us → Book a Walkthrough →") is a nice touch but the copy "Tell us a Golf Pro is using us" is confusing in the screenshot. If this is a referral mechanism, it needs clearer copy. If it's an announcement, it's distracting from the conversion goal.
- Six+ distinct CTAs on one page creates decision paralysis. Consider A/B testing a version with a maximum of 3 CTAs: one primary (Book a Walkthrough), one secondary (Start Free), and one content-offer (ROI Report to Board).
