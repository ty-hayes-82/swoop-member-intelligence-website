## The Due Diligence Gaps

A skeptical Club GM would leave this page and immediately research:

1. **"Who are the other 6 founding partner clubs?"** — The page admits to 7 customers but names only one (Pinetree Country Club, via Janice McDonald's testimonial). A buyer will Google Pinetree Country Club + Swoop to verify the relationship exists.

2. **"Who founded Swoop and what's their background?"** — No founder/team information is visible on the pricing page. A GM staking their job on a tech vendor wants to know: Are these club industry veterans? Former tech founders? First-time entrepreneurs? The About page link exists in the nav, but its absence from the pricing page means the buyer must go looking.

3. **"What does 'AI-powered member intelligence' actually DO technically?"** — The pricing tiers list features but don't explain the underlying methodology. How does Swoop identify "at-risk" members? What data inputs? What's the false positive rate? A $1,499/mo AI product with no explanation of its model is asking for blind faith.

4. **"What PMS/POS systems does Swoop actually integrate with?"** — The FAQ mentions "26 integrations across 12 categories" but doesn't name a single one. A GM running Jonas, Northstar, or Club Essentials needs to know compatibility before even booking a walkthrough. "See the Full Platform" link exists but the buyer shouldn't have to leave the pricing page to answer a fundamental compatibility question.

5. **"Is this company going to exist in 12 months?"** — Seven customers, a Vercel staging URL, and no press coverage or funding announcements visible on this page. A buyer will search Crunchbase, LinkedIn, and industry publications to assess company viability.

6. **"What does the data security setup actually look like?"** — The FAQ answers "Is my members' data secure?" but the visible answer mentions data isolation and compliance without naming specific certifications (SOC 2, GDPR, etc.). For clubs handling member PII and payment data, this matters enormously.

7. **"Can I talk to a reference customer who ISN'T Janice McDonald?"** — One testimonial is a starting point, not proof. A buyer spending $18K/year will want 2-3 reference calls with clubs of similar size and type.

8. **"What's the actual contract structure?"** — "Cancel any time" is stated, but is it month-to-month? Annual with early termination? What happens to my data if I leave? The truncated FAQ on cancellation leaves this critical question unanswered.
