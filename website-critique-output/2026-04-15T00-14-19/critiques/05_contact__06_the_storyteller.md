## The Rewrite List

### 1. Hero Subheadline — Tries to Do Too Much in One Paragraph

**Current:**
> "In 30 minutes, we load your tee-sheet data into Swoop and show you exactly where revenue is leaking and which members are quietly disengaging. You leave with a prioritized action list — not a pitch deck."

**Why it fails:** This is strong but it's doing four jobs — time commitment, process, two problem categories, and the deliverable. The last sentence also repeats the headline's "not a pitch deck" line, which dilutes the punch of both.

**Suggested rewrite:**
> "In 30 minutes, we connect to your tee sheet and POS, surface the revenue gaps and at-risk members your team can't see today, and hand you a ranked action list. The average club finds $40K+ in recoverable annual revenue on the first call."

*(Replace $40K with whatever the real number is. If you don't have a solid average, use the Torres proof point: "One GM found $18K in a single flagged member.")*

## The Missing Story

**What's absent: A before/after club scenario that makes the 30-minute call feel inevitable.**

The Robert Torres testimonial is excellent — "$18K in annual dues" saved from a single flagged member. But it's one data point sitting below the form. What this page desperately needs is a **brief "day in the life" comparison** placed *above* or *alongside* the form:

> **Before Swoop:** "Your membership director reviews a spreadsheet of lapsed rounds. She flags 12 names. Three have already resigned. The board asks why no one caught it."
>
> **After Swoop:** "Swoop flags 23 at-risk members 90 days before they disengage. Your team intervenes with a dinner comp, a round invite, a personal call. Seventeen stay. That's $85K in retained dues this year."

This kind of concrete scenario does three things the current page doesn't: (1) it names the *person* in the club who feels the pain (membership director), making it real; (2) it gives the GM a story they can retell to their board to justify the call; and (3) it makes "30 minutes" feel like an absurdly small investment relative to the outcome.

The Torres quote hints at this story but doesn't dramatize it. One testimonial is proof. A scenario is *identification*. This page has proof. It needs identification.

A secondary missing element: **a second testimonial from a different club profile** (e.g., a larger equity club, or a smaller semi-private club) to widen the net. Right now, a GM at a 600-member club might wonder: "Meridian Hills is 340 members — does this work at our scale?" A second voice answers that question before it's asked.
