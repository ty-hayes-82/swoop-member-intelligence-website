## Trust Builders (What's Working)

*   **Radical Integration Transparency:** The "28 Integrations Across 10 Categories" section is a masterclass in B2B trust. By explicitly naming legacy and modern systems (Jonas, ForeUP, ClubEssentials, Toast, ADP) and acknowledging the pain of "legacy on-premise systems like Jonas," Swoop proves they actually understand the messy reality of club IT. 
*   **Upfront Pricing:** "Starting at $499/mo." right in the hero section immediately answers the buyer's first unasked question and qualifies the lead. It eliminates the "Contact Us for Pricing" black box that plagues B2B SaaS.
*   **Aggressive Security Posture:** The footer is packed with high-value B2B compliance signals: "Mutual NDA on every engagement. AES-256 at rest. TLS 1.3 in transit. SOC 2 Type II (Audit Active)." Furthermore, they explicitly state they use "read-only API," which instantly lowers the perceived risk of data corruption for an IT Director.
*   **Hyper-Specific Domain Language:** The copy doesn't use generic SaaS jargon. Phrases like "Stop leaving covers on the table," "Avg $2,400 recovered per flag," and "predict cancellations 24-72 hours ahead" demonstrate profound industry expertise. The comparison table directly addressing ClubEssentials + Spreadsheets vs. Swoop is highly credible.
*   **Operational Clarity:** Stating "Typical launch: 10 business days" and "No operational downtime" gives the buyer a clear, bounded expectation of the onboarding risk.

## Trust Killers (What's Hurting)

*   **The Ghost Town Effect (No Human Proof):** For a platform claiming "$251K Annualized Impact," there is not a single quote, photograph, or video from a real Club GM, Membership Director, or COO. In the private club industry, peer validation is everything. 
*   **Synthetic-Feeling Case Examples:** The "Take a dollar number to the board" section uses an example of "Karen Wittman, nine years." Because it lacks a last name, title, or club association, a skeptic assumes this is a synthesized marketing persona rather than a real saved member. 
*   **"Asterisk" Metrics:** The impressive numbers in the six feature cards (e.g., $14,200 dues protected) are tied to a tiny gray footnote: "* METRICS REPRESENT AVERAGE IMPACT OBSERVED AT A 500-MEMBER CLUB OVER A TRAILING 90-DAY PERIOD." While I appreciate the baseline context, without naming the clubs in the data set, it borders on "trust us" math. They do name *one* pilot ("Pinetree CC pilot"), but it is buried in small text.
*   **Faceless Corporation:** There is no "Team" section visible on this page. If I am handing over my entire club's proprietary member data, POS data, and tee sheet data, I want to know exactly whose hands it is in. A Charleston address (1240 Broad Street) in the footer is good, but human faces are better.
