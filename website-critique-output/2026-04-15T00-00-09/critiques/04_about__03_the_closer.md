**1. Anchor the Hero CTA to the Bottom Form (Impact: High / Effort: Low)**
Currently, clicking "Book a Walkthrough" in the hero likely loads a new page. Since you have a perfectly optimized 3-field form right at the bottom of *this* page, anchor the top CTA to smooth-scroll down to the bottom form. Reduce page loads, reduce friction.

**2. Unify the "Founding Partner" CTA (Impact: High / Effort: Low)**
Update the "Claim a Founding Partner Spot" button to say "Book a Walkthrough to Claim Your Spot." This removes cognitive load. The visitor instantly understands that the walkthrough *is* the mechanism to get one of the 3 remaining spots, leveraging the scarcity without confusing the user flow.

**3. Elevate the Routing Links to Secondary Buttons (Impact: Medium / Effort: Low)**
The text link "See how the platform works →" (under the Moat section) and "Ask us for a direct reference call..." (under the Testimonials) are practically invisible. Turn them into ghost buttons (outlined, transparent background). Right now, users who are interested but not quite ready to book a walkthrough will scroll right past their ideal micro-conversion paths.

**4. Add a GM/COO Identifier to the Hero Headline (Impact: Medium / Effort: Low)**
The headline is great, but make the target explicitly clear to increase self-identification. Tweak the eyebrow or subhead to read: "ABOUT SWOOP: The operating system for Private Club GMs & COOs." 

**5. Make the Reference Call Link a Trigger (Impact: Low / Effort: Medium)**
The copy "Ask us for a direct reference call with a founding-partner GM" is a massive trust signal. Don't make it a static text link. Make it a clickable element that either opens a mailto: link pre-filled with a request, or opens a simple 2-field modal (Email + "Request Reference Call"). Make it frictionless for skeptical buyers to take that step.
