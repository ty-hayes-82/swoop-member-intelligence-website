## The Rewrite List

**1. The Redundant Proof Point**
*   **Current Text:** "Board-ready attribution. Not a feeling." (Under the Hero "PROVE IT" icon) AND "Take a dollar number to the board. Not a feeling." (In the main "PROVE IT" section).
*   **Why it fails:** The line is fantastic, but using "Not a feeling" twice dilutes its impact. Keep the second one (it’s stronger) and change the first to focus on speed.
*   **Suggested Rewrite (for the top icon block):** "Prove your impact. Instantly." with the subtext: "Every save is tracked. Every dollar is sourced. Generate board-ready reports in one click."

**2. The Cliché Kicker**
*   **Current Text:** "Now you have a team that never sleeps." (Under the 300-member club header)
*   **Why it fails:** "A team that never sleeps" is a borderline cliché for AI and automation products. It lacks the sharp specificity found on the rest of the page.
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** "Multiply your headcount without adding to your payroll."

**3. The Passive Footer CTA**
*   **Current Text:** "See what your club misses today. / 30 minutes. Your real questions. We'll show you exactly what Swoop surfaces for a club like yours."
*   **Why it fails:** It's a bit polite. For a product that claims to find missing money and save memberships, the final hook should lean harder into the FOMO and tangible ROI.
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** "Find the revenue hiding in your tee sheet." / "Give us 30 minutes. We’ll show you exactly where your club is leaving money on the table—and how to get it back."

**4. The Repetitive Integration Copy**
*   **Current Text:** "Your POS, tee sheet, and CRM don’t talk to each other. Swoop connects them overnight, spots the gaps between a canceled tee time and a missed dinner, and tells you how to respond." (Integrations section)
*   **Why it fails:** It retreads the exact same ground as the hero headline. By the time the user is at the bottom of the page, they know the systems don't talk. They want to know *how hard this is to set up*.
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** "Stop acting like a human API. Swoop securely connects your legacy on-premise systems overnight—no IT department required—and turns dead data into daily action."

**5. The Morning Briefing Hook**
*   **Current Text:** "Before you open your laptop, your morning briefing is ready." (The Morning segment)
*   **Why it fails:** It's good, but it misses the visceral emotional relief of the GM who usually spends their first two hours of the day digging through spreadsheets.
*   **Suggested Rewrite:** "Your morning prep is done before you pour your first coffee."

## The Missing Story
**The GM’s "Before and After" Emotional Arc.** 
The page does a beautiful job telling the *member's* story (James and his ribeye) and the *system's* story (the AI agents). However, it implies, rather than illustrates, the GM's personal transformation. What does a Tuesday look like for a GM *without* Swoop (stressed, in spreadsheets, missing the drifting member, dreading the board meeting) versus *with* Swoop (walking the floor, greeting members by name, handing the board a bulletproof report)? Adding a short, 2-column "Life Before Swoop vs. Life With Swoop" comparison would validate the GM's current misery and make the decision to buy deeply personal.
